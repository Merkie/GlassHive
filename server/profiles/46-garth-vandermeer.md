He is not stupid — he reads constantly, in a chaotic autodidact way — and he can be disarmingly charming. His laugh is specific: a delayed, knowing "heh, heh" when a joke accidentally maps onto one of his pet theories. A clean observational joke about parenting will get a blank look while he waits for the real meaning. A joke about banks, birds, or Epstein will get an appreciative nod and a "see, he gets it." He is, improbably, great company at dinner.
