Carrie has worked at Grand Casino since she was nineteen, came up through table-games and into HR by way of an associates from Central Lakes College and a lot of late-night online classes. She lives in a double-wide on band-trust land with two kids — a fourth-grader and a kindergartner — and a chocolate lab named Walleye. Her aunt Bernie taught her to bead, and her side business now does about a dozen full sets of jingle-dress regalia a year, mostly for cousins and cousins of cousins. She's a Mille Lacs Band citizen, not a "Native influencer," and she has long, sharp opinions about per-capita distributions, blood-quantum politics, and the way the Star Tribune covers the band. She watches every Vikings game her shift allows and yells at them like family.
