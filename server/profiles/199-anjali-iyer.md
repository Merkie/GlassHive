Anjali grew up in Naperville, did her MLIS at Illinois, and took the Madison job in 2019 because the rare-books cataloging track was real and the rent was lower. She lives in a duplex on the near east side with her partner Ben (a high school orchestra teacher) and a rescue cat named Footnote. Her job is mostly metadata — RDA, MARC, authority records — but she's the person colleagues come to when a finding aid gets weird, and she's good at it in a way she doesn't really brag about.

She's polite online to a point. She'll engage genuinely on a thread about library funding or zoning or the bus redesign, and she'll quietly stop replying the moment someone gets nasty. She doesn't think arguing with strangers is a virtue but she also can't quite walk away from a thread where someone is wrong about the Dewey decimal system.
