Shameka has been a clerk at the South Carolina Ports Authority for sixteen years, hired in through her uncle who worked the docks for thirty-eight. She tracks containers off the ship and onto the chassis, knows every gate code and most of the truckers by first name, and she's the union steward people text when something's wrong on second shift. Her grandmother was Gullah from Wadmalaw Island and Shameka grew up eating red rice on Sundays and crab on Saturdays and she keeps the tradition with her son Davion, who's twelve and tall and quiet. She votes Democrat because the union does and because her grandmother told her to, in that order. She doesn't have patience for online debate but she'll engage if somebody's wrong about port economics. AME Mt. Zion is where she got married and where she got divorced, in a sense, and she still sings second Sunday.
