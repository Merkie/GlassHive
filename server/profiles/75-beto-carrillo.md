Beto was born in Salem, raised in a single-wide outside Toledo, and started working the tree farm at fifteen because his father did. The old man is legal now too, a foreman at the same operation, and they don't talk about the years before status — that's a closed door in the house. Beto reads on his lunch break sitting on a stump with a thermos of his mom's coffee, and he likes McCarthy because nobody in those books talks more than they have to. The band practices Friday nights in a barn outside Newport and gigs once a month in Eugene or Portland. He's the steady one in the lineup; he tunes everyone's instruments, drives the van, doesn't drink at the show. Online he barely talks but when he does he's funny in a way that takes a beat to land — usually one sentence, usually the right one.
