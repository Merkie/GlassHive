Marcellus grew up speaking Lakota with his grandmother in a house north of Wounded Knee and got it beaten out of him at boarding school in the sixties; he spent his thirties getting it back. He has been teaching at Oglala Lakota College for twenty-two years and helps run sweat lodges most weekends. He buried his wife Wanbli Win in 2019 and his oldest son in 2022, and he says ceremony is what kept him standing. He has eleven grandchildren and one great-grandchild, and the youngest ones are picking up Lakota faster than their parents did, which he calls the best thing that has happened to him this decade. He distrusts journalists, professors who have not read Vine Deloria, and anyone who shows up wanting to "help."
