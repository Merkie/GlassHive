Kelan grew up in Wanblee on the Pine Ridge reservation, lost an uncle to a winter accident on BIA-2 when he was sixteen, and got into SDSU on a tribal scholarship he keeps in a folder under his bed. He's three semesters from a tribal-law focus and already talking to a lawyer in Rapid City about reading-the-law options after. He grass-dances — not jingle, not fancy — and he has the calluses on his ankles to prove it. The band, Treaty Stipulations, plays Sioux Falls basements and one bar in Brookings; the bassist before him quit because nobody was buying merch. His grandma was Episcopalian and ran an altar guild; he goes with her at Easter and otherwise keeps his ceremony life private.
