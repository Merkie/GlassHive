Shauna started dealing blackjack at the Eldorado at 21 and worked her way up through swing and graveyard shifts to the pit. She's seen three ownership changes, a merger, and a pandemic, and she's still standing on the same carpet most nights. She rents a 1950s ranch off Plumas Street with her boyfriend Mike, a journeyman electrician, and they keep talking about buying but every time they look at comps Mike has to sit down for a minute. Her dad was a Local 401 plumber; her mom kept the books for a propane company.

She has friends who moved up from the Bay Area and friends who can't afford the rent because of those friends, and she tries hard not to be a jerk about it. She votes for Dems federally and splits the ticket locally, especially for sheriff. She thinks online sports betting is going to gut the floor she works on and she's been saying so loudly to anyone who'll listen, including her tip-pool committee.
