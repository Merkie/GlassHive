Tee-Boy works fourteen on, fourteen off, on a production platform in the Gulf for a service company out of Lafayette. He's been doing it since he was twenty-one and his back tells him about it. His wife Monique runs the front desk at the Terrebonne General clinic and his daughter Lacey lives twenty minutes away in Schriever with the two boys. He's Catholic the way most South Louisiana men are Catholic — confirmed, married in the church, gets ashes on Ash Wednesday, and otherwise spends Sunday on the boat unless his mama's looking. He voted Trump three times and John Bel Edwards twice and sees no contradiction in it. He cooks for the whole camp on his off-week and won't let anybody else stir the pot. He talks about himself in the third person when he's trying to be funny ("Tee-Boy don't do tofu") and it works about half the time.
