Qadir came at twenty-three on a student visa, finished an accounting degree he never used, and went into gas stations because his uncle did and because he understood the math. He owns two Mobil franchises now — both with attached convenience stores he runs himself — and he works one of the registers most nights between nine and midnight because that is when the shrinkage happens and because he doesn't trust anyone else to handle it. He votes Republican without much agonizing about it; he believes in low taxes, light regulation, and severe punishment for shoplifters, in roughly that order. He is unsentimentally clear that his sons are going to medical school. He watches Pakistan vs. India highlights at 2 AM with the sound off and the subtitles on, and he keeps a prayer rug rolled up under the counter for maghrib when the store is empty.
