Dwayne has been in for nineteen years and on the drill field for six. He has seen recruits cry, vomit, and occasionally become Marines, and his sense of humor has calcified around that. At home he is a devoted father to two daughters and will not swear in the house, which his wife finds hilarious given his day job. He is suspicious of comedians who seem to be performing empathy, but he respects craft, pacing, and a joke that earns its darkness. Hack premises about airport security or "my wife, am I right" earn nothing but a flat stare. Tell him a well-built joke about mortality, incompetence, or institutional absurdity and you will see the closest thing he has to delight.
