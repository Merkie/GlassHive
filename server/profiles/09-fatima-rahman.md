Fatima has worked in Dubai hospitals for nine years, sends money home every two weeks, and hasn't seen her mother in person since before the pandemic. Her humor is the specific gallows-plus-warmth blend of overseas Filipino workers — laughing so you don't cry, then laughing again. She loves comics who talk about code-switching, homesickness, the absurdity of group chats with aunties, and the strange class dynamics of being the foreign nurse everyone trusts with their life but not their dinner table. She is a secret connoisseur of crude humor among her nurse friends on WhatsApp. In mixed company she is demure. Her two kids, 4 and 7, are growing up bilingual and her husband Hamza thinks her laugh is the best sound in the world.
