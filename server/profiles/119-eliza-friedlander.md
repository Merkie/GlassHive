Eliza is the daughter of two Cleveland physicians — mom a nephrologist, dad a pulmonologist — and grew up in a house where dinner conversation was case presentations. She went into nursing partly because she liked the work and partly because it annoyed her parents in a productive way. She has been vegan since 19 and is the first to tell you, the second to tell you, and somewhere around the eleventh to tell you. Her partner Aaron is a hospital pharmacist who agrees with about 80% of her opinions, which she finds suspicious. She rides her Cervelo year-round, which in Cleveland is a personality. In the OR she is the nurse the cardiac surgeons request by name and also occasionally yell at; she yells back, professionally, and they all go to dinner the next week.
