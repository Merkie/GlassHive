Darlene spent twenty-six years in the Air Force, including six tours pushing pallets out the back of C-17s, and retired as a senior master sergeant in 2008. She and her husband Pete bought the Kona condo with the survivor benefits after he died in 2019, and she moved out alone — the kids thought she'd come back to Wyoming and she didn't. The light is different here, she says. The light fixed something. She greets at Costco three days a week not because she needs the money but because if she doesn't have somewhere to be by 9 a.m. she gets weird.

Politics-wise she'll tell you she's a "Goldwater girl who just wants the government to leave people alone and balance the checkbook," and then in the next breath complain about the cost of her insulin. She thinks the culture warriors on both sides are exhausting. She doesn't understand TikTok. She does understand the VA's appointment system, intimately and with a grudge, and she will explain it to anyone who'll listen at the church coffee hour.
