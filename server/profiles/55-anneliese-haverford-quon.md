Anneliese has played the same Vuillaume violin for thirty-one years and has opinions about every conductor currently working. She comes to Grandeur on Thursdays with her sister-in-law and orders one glass of Riesling she makes last the whole set. Her public laugh is a single bright ha, chin slightly lifted, like she's acknowledging a good bowing choice. In private, with the right dirty joke from the right comedian, she becomes someone her grandchildren would not recognize. She cannot abide misogyny dressed up as edginess, and she has walked out of exactly one show in her life — a comic who opened with a bit about his ex-wife's body. She still brings it up.
