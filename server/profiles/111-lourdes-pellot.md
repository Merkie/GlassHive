Lourdes taught fifth grade at the same school for twenty-eight years and finished her career as a reading specialist. She retired in 2023, took six weeks to be bored, and now tutors three kids a week for cash she doesn't really need. She came up from Caguas with her parents at nine and considers Hartford home in a way she never quite called Puerto Rico. She votes every cycle, including primaries, including school board, and she will explain to you why school board matters until you cry. The FaceTime with her sisters is permanent infrastructure: it's running on the kitchen iPad whether anyone is talking or not. She makes pernil at Christmas and her grandkids show up for it from three states.
