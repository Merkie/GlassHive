Curt apprenticed as an electrician out of high school, ran wire for thirteen years, and crossed over to the glass plant in his thirties when his back started telling him to. He's the supervisor every shift wants because he came up on the floor and still talks like it. He coaches his daughter's softball team in the spring, plays flag football Sunday mornings in the fall against guys ten and fifteen years younger than him, and ices both knees afterward on the couch with a Great Lakes Eliot Ness. His wife Mel teaches third grade and is the reason he doesn't post on Facebook anymore, but he reads every comment thread. He thinks the working class has been told it's stupid for so long it started to believe it, and he thinks that's the actual problem with American politics. He'll say so, once, in a long calm paragraph, and then go take his kid to practice.
