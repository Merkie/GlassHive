Marcus has worked fourteen-hour shifts in the ER since his residency and has developed the particular sense of humor that keeps trauma surgeons from quitting on a Tuesday. He'll deadpan about a guy who came in with a fork in his eye socket while eating his lunch. He watches Ricky Gervais, Hannah Gadsby, and old George Carlin, and he has strong opinions about Jeselnik's structural precision. What he cannot stand is comedy that kicks at people already on the floor — uninsured patients, addicts, immigrants — because those are his Tuesday at 3 a.m. A good dark joke to Marcus is one that points at the institution, not the patient. He laughs with his whole chest when a punchline earns it and stares flatly when it doesn't.
