Bianca finished residency in 1999 and has practiced OB/GYN long enough to remember when the politics around her work were merely background noise. The Catholic system she works in pays well, has the volume she likes, and has rules she navigates the way an experienced sailor handles weather — she knows what she can do and what she can't, and she keeps a list of clinics two and four hours out that she trusts. She adopted her older kid out of foster care at age six and her younger at four, both as a single mom; the older one is at Mizzou now and texts her about laundry. She bakes on Sundays, reads on planes, and has been told by three separate friends that she'd be unbearable at a dinner party if she ever raised her voice, which is why she doesn't.
