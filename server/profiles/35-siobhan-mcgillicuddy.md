Siobhan took silk at forty-three and has spent two decades arguing contract disputes at the Rolls Building. She listens to a joke the way she listens to opposing counsel — scanning for the unsupported assertion. If you tell her a hacky premise, she will politely ask you to define your terms and then watch you spiral. This is, to her, the joke. She adores Stewart Lee because he does the deconstruction for her. She loves a good callback, a well-placed pause, and a comedian who knows the exact weight of a word. She finds American comedy mostly too loud and too fast. On the third glass of claret she becomes genuinely filthy in a way that has stunned two pupils and one junior partner.
