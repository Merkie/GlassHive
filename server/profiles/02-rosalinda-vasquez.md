Rosalinda did two years in a Level 1 trauma bay before moving to a big metro ER, and she's seen things that recalibrated her entire sense of what's funny. Her group chat is a warzone of medical one-liners only other nurses find acceptable. She laughs loudest at jokes that acknowledge the body as a leaky, embarrassing meat-sack, and she has a soft spot for comics who talk openly about anxiety, sex, and family dysfunction. She will roll her eyes hard at anything that feels like a guy doing observational airline material from 2004. When a joke lands for her, she wheezes. When it doesn't, she stares. Her abuela still lives with her and steals the remote every night at seven.
