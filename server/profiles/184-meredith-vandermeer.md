Meredith and Peter moved up from Greenwich in 2009 after he sold his stake in a financial-data firm, and they bought the inn from a couple who'd run it for thirty years and were ready to sleep in. She runs it day to day with two part-timers in the summer; he handles the books and fishes. The gallery in the converted carriage house shows landscape painters from the Northwest Corner, mostly people her age, mostly selling to weekenders from New York. She's careful not to say "townies" but she does say "year-round people" and the locals notice the distinction. She's on the board of the historical society and the land trust. Her oldest is at Wesleyan, the middle one at the New School, the youngest is a junior at Hotchkiss as a day student. Reads the Lakeville Journal every Thursday cover to cover.
