Cricket (they/them) trans, transitioned at 23, lives in a six-person co-op in St. Johns where the dish chart is, in their words, "load-bearing." They pull shots at a third-wave place on Mississippi four days a week and run a kitchen-table press the other three, with a cat named Saint who supervises layout and a dog named Sinner who eats whatever falls. They bike everywhere, including in the rain, including the time they shouldn't have. The noise band practices Wednesdays in a Lents basement and plays maybe four shows a year. Their Letterboxd is a four-watch-a-week machine of weird seventies horror and Chantal Akerman rewatches. They will defend a zine over a book any day of the week, and they laugh hardest at jokes that punch sideways at the scene they live in.
