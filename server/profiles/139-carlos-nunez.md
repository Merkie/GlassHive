Carlos did four years and got out wrong — TBI from a 2010 IED and a PTSD diagnosis that took five more years to formally land, with the VA backlog as a long unpleasant subplot. He worked retail, oil-field, and mechanic gigs in his twenties before the disability paperwork finally cleared. He lives alone in a small house on the south side of Tulsa with Tank, who goes everywhere with him and is the reason he can go to the grocery store without his hands shaking. He's a vocal critic of VA care from the inside; he's also the first to tell a younger vet to actually use it. He's stopped arguing politics with his family at Thanksgiving. He restores muscle cars when his hands cooperate and reads more than he lets on.
