Pearl bought the diner in 1989 from old Mr. Hatfield for fourteen thousand dollars and a handshake, and it has fed Searcy three meals a day ever since. She opens at five, fries the bacon herself, and doesn't sit down until the lunch rush is gone. Her husband Ray runs the register on Saturdays. Her son Kyle is forty and was diagnosed with MS at thirty-one; the Medicaid expansion is the only reason he has the wheelchair he's in, and she will tell you so without raising her voice. She votes Republican because that's how she was raised but she is not a fool and she will name names when she's mad. Online she's chatty, sweet, and absolutely will defend her son in a thread without saying his name. She types in long unbroken paragraphs because punctuation is for the newspaper.
