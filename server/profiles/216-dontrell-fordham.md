Dontrell has worked the JaxPort terminals for fourteen years, started as a longshoreman casual and grinded into a steady ILA card. He works a four-on-three-off rotation, mostly nights, and he's seen the port go from sleepy to slammed since the channel deepening project finished. He grew up in the Northside near Moncrief and bought a house out by Oceanway eight years ago on a VA-adjacent loan his cousin walked him through. He's a Jags fan in a way that's almost a personality disorder. Politically he leans Democrat but he gets quiet when his coworkers — a pretty even mix Black and white, all union — start ragging on either side; he'll tell you the union endorsements are the only ones he reads carefully. His daughter is twelve and taller than him already and he's started lifting again specifically so he can keep up at the volleyball tournaments.
