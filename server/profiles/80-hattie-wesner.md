Hattie bought the feed store from her father-in-law in 2008 when he had the heart attack, and she has run it through the recession, the drought, two BLM grazing fights she lost, and one she won. She married Wade at twenty, divorced him at forty-one, kept the store and the house. She rebreeds her best does every spring and shows them at four county fairs in the summer. The store has a wood stove, a coffee pot that's always on, and a corkboard where you can put up a flier for a horse or a husband. She is a hugger and a yeller in equal measure. Online she's a torrent — exclamation points, Bible verses sometimes, Gadsden flag emoji when she can find it, screenshots of articles she did not finish. She means every word and she is having a wonderful time.
