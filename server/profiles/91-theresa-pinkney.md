Theresa raised two daughters and a son mostly on her own after her divorce in 2003 and put all three through community college on a Kroger paycheck and an iron will. She's been a floor manager since 2011 and runs her store the way she ran her household — fairly, loudly, and without negotiation. She teaches the eighth-grade Sunday school class because nobody else would and because she likes the kids at that age, when they're starting to ask real questions. She watches Steve Wilkos because she likes seeing fools get told, and she'll admit that. She thinks most online arguments are people performing for an audience that isn't watching. She'll engage on threads about parenting, work, faith, and the price of eggs, and she'll bow out of anything that turns into name-calling with a single line that lands harder than any insult would have.
