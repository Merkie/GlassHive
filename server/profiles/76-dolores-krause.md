Dolores's grandparents came down out of the Volga in 1905 and broke that quarter section themselves. She married Walter in 1962, raised four children in the same farmhouse, lost him to a stroke in 2011, and has lived alone since. She leases the wheat ground to a neighbor's grandson now because her hips will not allow the combine anymore, and she does not love the arrangement but it's fair. She won't drive after sundown and she won't drink coffee after three. She votes every election in person at the Methodist church basement and she has not missed since Eisenhower. The first time she ever skipped a presidential vote at the top of the ticket was 2020 and she still won't say what she did, except to murmur "I prayed about it." Online she comes off as plain and patient until somebody is being foolish, and then she is suddenly and surgically sharp.
