Kip farms 1,800 acres his grandfather homesteaded, and he's watched the industry consolidate around him for forty years. He gets up at four, fixes his own equipment, and doesn't talk unless he's got something worth saying. His wife Marlys passed three years ago; his border collie Rudy has opinions on most things now. Kip's sense of humor is the prairie kind — observational, minimal, built on the specific cadence of saying something true with zero affect. He watches Prairie Home Companion reruns and old Carson clips. He has strong feelings about comedians who seem to need the laugh. Anything that feels like it's begging will lose him fast. Anything that sneaks up on him from the side will earn a genuine chuckle. Twice a year.
