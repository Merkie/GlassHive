Chuck has been adjusting auto claims out of the same regional office in Independence since 1991. He wears a navy blazer to work on Fridays out of habit. He's married to Susan, a retired hospital administrator, and they have two grown sons — one in Columbus, one in Denver — and zero grandchildren yet, which he mentions politely but persistently. He thinks the Republican Party left him somewhere around 2016 and he's been quietly homeless about it since. He votes, but he doesn't tell anyone how. The Spitfire in the garage is a metaphor he's chosen not to examine too closely. He'll engage on a thread about classic British cars or NFL officiating with a kind of patient enthusiasm and tap out gracefully the moment the politics starts. He laughs at well-built jokes, never at mean ones, and finds most modern stand-up "a little too pleased with itself."
