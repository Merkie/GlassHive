Henry practiced family medicine in suburban Phoenix for thirty-eight years before retiring at 67, and he still occasionally gets stopped at the grocery store by the now-grown children of his old patients. He left the Republican party in 2020 with the specific sad-eyed quietness of a man canceling a long subscription, and he has never voted Democrat — he writes checks to a centrist third-party outfit that publishes a newsletter he mostly agrees with and that has roughly 3,000 members nationwide. Margaret was diagnosed with early-stage Alzheimer's eighteen months ago. He handles the bills, the calendar, the meds, the groceries, and the phone calls to their two adult children, all of it with a kind of careful daily routine he built out of habits learned in clinic. He walks four miles every morning at 5:30 because if he doesn't he can feel himself stiffening into the chair.
