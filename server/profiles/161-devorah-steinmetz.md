Devorah runs the catering out of an extended kitchen in her Williamsburg apartment, with a walk-in fridge in what used to be a closet and a payment system run through her husband's phone because she doesn't carry one of her own. On a busy week she's cooking for three sheva brachas, a vort, and a kiddush, with two of her older daughters plating and a sister-in-law on deliveries. She reads Mishpacha cover to cover on Shabbos, and during the week she lives inside the WhatsApp group of Satmar women caterers who keep each other alive through wholesale tips and venue gossip. She has eight kids, the oldest a seventeen-year-old who already knows how to feed a wedding, and a toddler who lives on her hip.
