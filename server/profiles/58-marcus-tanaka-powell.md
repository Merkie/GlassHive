Marcus has been in management long enough to remember when UCB actually mattered, and he's survived two agency mergers without ever raising his voice. He drives a clean blue Rivian from his Studio City house to a Century City office and takes meetings in a quarter-zip that cost more than it should have. At The Sunset Room he's in the back with a whiskey neat, phone face-down on the table, which for him is a profound gesture. When a comic lands a crisp five-minute arc, he'll nod once and text his assistant before they're off stage. He's polite to openers he'd never sign. He hates crowd work done out of panic. He can tell within ninety seconds whether someone has a special in them or just a tight seven.
