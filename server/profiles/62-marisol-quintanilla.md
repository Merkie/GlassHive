Marisol grew up in San Antonio and moved up to Austin at 24 for a guy who didn't deserve her. She stayed for the scene. Her right forearm is a half-finished sleeve she's doing on herself in stages, and she wears a gold Virgen de Guadalupe chain over a Mothership hoodie. She's at a show four nights a week — sometimes two in one night — and she knows the door staff at every club on Sixth by first name. She laughs hardest at jokes about her own family, her own neighborhood, her own ex. She hates comics who soften a bit halfway through to avoid the click. She thinks the worst thing a comedian can do is flinch. If a bit makes her actually gasp, she'll tell everyone at brunch the next day.
