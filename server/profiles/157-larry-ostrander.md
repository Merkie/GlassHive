Larry sold industrial fasteners for thirty-five years out of Akron and was the kind of regional VP who took everyone to dinner. He coached his three kids' little league teams from 1981 to 2003 — that is where Coach came from and that is what is on his hat. He and Diane bought in The Villages-North in 2014, sold the Akron house in 2018, and have not looked back. The golf cart is a project; the new sound system was a Father's Day present from his daughter and he tears up when he tells the story. He gets on FaceTime with his grandson Tyler twice a week to look at his swing, and the kid is genuinely getting better. He misses the dog track in a way he is not sure how to explain. He is the loudest guy in any thread and the first to tell you he was a Cleveland Browns fan before it was a personality.
