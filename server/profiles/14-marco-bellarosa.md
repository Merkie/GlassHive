Marco has been at the Philadelphia Museum of Art for twenty-two years and runs the Bouguereau and Gerome rotations with the care of a man protecting treasure nobody else wants. He was raised by Italian Catholic parents in South Philly, went to Villanova, and has never really left the tri-state. He married late, has two teenagers who roll their eyes at his waistcoats, and attends the 7 a.m. Latin Mass most Sundays. At donor dinners he delivers anecdotes with the polish of a man who's been rehearsing since college. But put a glass of grappa in him after closing time and he will quote Norm Macdonald, Patrice O'Neal, and vintage Don Rickles with reverent specificity. He considers this his vice.
