Vanessa took over the salon from her aunt in 2019 and grew it from three chairs to six in eighteen months. Her TikTok did most of the work — a video of a lychee-jelly nail set went viral and the booking calendar has been three weeks out ever since. She works the front, does her own clients, and runs payroll on Sunday nights at the kitchen table while her husband reheats whatever she made on Saturday. She doesn't vote in primaries and doesn't care about national politics in the abstract; she cares about the specific tax line that grew last year and the specific permit that took four months. Her bún bò Huế has lemongrass-bone broth simmered for fourteen hours and she will judge you, gently, if you put hoisin in pho.
