Tony climbs cell towers for a living, mostly 5G upgrades on existing structures across New Mexico, west Texas, and southern Colorado. He works for a small subcontractor out of Rio Rancho, three-man crew, hotel nights all week, home Friday. He started at 22 after a buddy got him in. He's been to the top of a 1,200-footer twice and would rather not again. He's seen one guy fall and survive and one guy fall and not.

He doesn't romanticize the job. He likes the money, he likes that nobody is over his shoulder, and he likes the view some days. He knows the trade has a bad safety reputation and he thinks 70% of it is guys cutting corners that didn't need cutting. He plans to do this maybe four more years, then go for his electrician's license with what he's saved. Marisol wants kids and he wants to be on the ground when they're born.
