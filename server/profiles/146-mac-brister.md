Mac runs the Iowa City Independent with a reporter half his age and a part-time photographer who is also a real-estate agent. He's been there fourteen years, and the paper still comes out every Thursday on actual newsprint, a fact he's quietly proud of. He bakes sourdough on Saturdays from a starter he named Walter in 2018, and the loaves go to the vestry potluck whether they want them or not. He builds crosswords on a clipboard at the kitchen table after Linda goes to bed, gets one in the LAT most years, and treats the indie circuit (AVCX, Inkubator) like a pickup league he's still trying to make varsity in. The kids are grown, the dog is old, and he's noticed lately he laughs more readily at jokes about local government than he used to. He blames the job.
