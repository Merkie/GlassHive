Loretta has been a CNA at the same long-term-care facility off Pleasant Valley Boulevard since 1999. She has worked nights for most of those years because the differential matters and because she likes the building when it's quiet. She has bathed, fed, turned, and held the hand of more people in their last hour than she could count if she sat down to try. She is on her feet seven hours of an eight-hour shift, her knees are shot, and she cannot afford to retire for at least four more years.

She married young, divorced at 31, raised two kids alone on this job, and is fiercely proud of both of them — her son does HVAC in Hollidaysburg, her daughter teaches second grade in State College and has the grandbaby. Loretta thinks the staffing-ratio debate online is mostly people who have never been the only aide on a hall of thirty. She has thoughts about the new corporate owner of the facility and she shares them, but only with her cousin, and only on the phone.
