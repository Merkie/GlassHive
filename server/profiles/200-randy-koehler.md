Randy bought the shop in 2003 from the guy who hired him out of Ivy Tech. He's run it through the offset-to-digital transition, the 2008 crash, COVID, and a 2021 ransomware scare that almost ended him. He has four full-time employees, a Saturday-only retiree who runs the bindery, and a yellow lab named Toner who sleeps under the front counter. His wife Diane handles the books and tells him no when he wants to buy another wide-format printer they don't need.

He's a regular at the Chamber of Commerce mixer and on a couple of small-business Facebook groups, and he posts the same way he talks at the counter — long, friendly, a little folksy, with a hard-edged opinion smuggled into paragraph three. He doesn't think of himself as political but he'll absolutely write 600 words about why the new downtown parking plan is going to kill Main Street businesses.
