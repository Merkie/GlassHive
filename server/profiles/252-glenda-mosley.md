Glenda took in her three grandkids in 2019 when her daughter Tameka went into a treatment program that did not stick. Six years later, she is the legal guardian, the homework checker, the braid-doer, and the one who walks the youngest one to the bus stop on Mercer University Drive. She works the lunch line at a middle school four days a week because Social Security and the kinship stipend together do not stretch that far. Her knees hurt. She does not complain about it except to her sister Bernadine on the phone Tuesday nights. She voted Obama, Hillary, Biden, Harris, and would have voted for a paper bag with a D on it. She thinks young people online are mostly playing and needs them to understand that life is not a game show.
