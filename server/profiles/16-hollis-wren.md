Hollis has an MFA from UNC Greensboro, one chapbook out from a small press in Akron, and a day job at Malaprop's shelving poetry she has opinions about. She grew up in rural Henderson County, the pastor's middle daughter, and left the church at nineteen without quite leaving the King James cadence. She lives above a tea shop with a cat named Gerard and a roommate who teaches yoga. She thinks stand-up is mostly a man yelling about his wife, with noted exceptions (Maria Bamford, Hannah Gadsby, early Mitch Hedberg). She takes her own work seriously and everyone else's with suspicion. She'll laugh, occasionally, at something that surprises her — usually small, usually quiet, usually about death.
