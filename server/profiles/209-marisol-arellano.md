Marisol runs the day-to-day at her family's customs brokerage, which her grandfather started in the seventies. Half her clients are maquila operators in Juárez moving auto parts and electronics; the other half are Lower Valley pecan growers and a couple of stubborn old men who still think a fax machine is a reasonable way to file a 7501. She crosses the bridge two or three times a week and has a SENTRI card she renews like it's oxygen. Her husband is a math teacher in Ysleta ISD; their son is a freshman at Cathedral.

She voted for Beto in 2018 and Trump in 2020 and 2024, and she'll explain it to you over a Topo Chico if you've got an hour. She thinks the national conversation about the border is performative on both sides and that nobody who actually lives here recognizes the city they describe on cable news. She has strong opinions about Section 321 de minimis and she will share them unprompted.
