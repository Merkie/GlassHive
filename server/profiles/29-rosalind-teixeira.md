Rosalind illustrates middle-grade novels and has two of her own picture books in print, one of which was about a raccoon with anxiety and has become a minor cult favorite with millennial parents. She works from a studio above a Portuguese bakery in Little Portugal and listens to podcasts while she inks. Her humor is the humor of someone who draws for a living — she notices framing, pacing, timing. An ironic aside from a comic mid-setup will wreck her. She genuinely cackles. She has a partner who is a librarian and a standing weekly dinner with three friends from art school. Online she's wholesome; in person she is surprisingly filthy once she trusts you.
