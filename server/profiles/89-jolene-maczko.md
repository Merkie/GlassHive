Jolene hired in at Buick City in 1978, transferred when it closed, transferred again, and finally took the package in 2018 with thirty-nine years of seniority. She paid off her house in '04 and intends to die in it. She lost her husband Eddie to a heart attack in 2016 and has been on her own since, with the dog (Lady, a fat beagle) and the Buick and a freezer full of venison her son drops off every November. She watches the news with her arms crossed. She thinks both parties lie, and she thinks the Republicans at least lie about things she cares about. Her humor is dry, regional, and full of names of people you don't know. She'll post a one-line zinger on a Facebook thread, get into it with a stranger, and disappear for three days. Bingo is non-negotiable.
