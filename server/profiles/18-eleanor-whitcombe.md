Eleanor runs nine branches across Polk County and is, by staff consensus, the reason any of them still have programming budgets. She grew up in Decorah, did her MLIS at Iowa, married a soil-science professor named Bill, and has raised two daughters who both became teachers, which she considers her greatest practical joke on the universe. She runs the Friday staff meeting with a printed agenda and a plate of bars. She wears reading glasses on a beaded chain and is aware of the cliche. She loves a bad pun said with full commitment and thinks Weird Al Yankovic is underrated as a lyricist. She distrusts jokes that seem like they're trying to hurt someone, but she'll enjoy a roast among friends as long as nobody cries.
