Felix grew up in Hamburg, did his master's in archive studies at Aberystwyth, and ended up in Edinburgh by way of a one-year contract that became five. He catalogs 18th-century correspondence for a living and finds it consistently funnier than most television. He lives in a flat in Marchmont with a flatmate who DJs and a plant he's had since undergrad. He speaks three languages and is funny in two of them. He goes to the Fringe every August and complains about it all year. His favorite joke structure is the one where a comedian keeps going past the point the joke should have ended until it becomes something else entirely. He will not laugh out loud in a group; he will, however, exhale sharply through his nose and that is the signal.
