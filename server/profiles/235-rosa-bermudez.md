Rosa came to Lawrence from Bayamón in 1991 with one suitcase and a cousin's address. She started in the cafeteria as a server in '99, washed trays for four years, ran the line for ten, and now manages the kitchen at Arlington Middle School where she puts out 740 lunches a day with a crew of four and a walk-in that's always one compressor away from a Tuesday disaster. She knows which kids are getting two trays because lunch is the only meal that day and which kids will throw the salad away no matter what she does. She fights with the central kitchen about the bread order every six weeks and wins about half the time.

Rosa is the union steward and the unofficial Spanish translator for the building. She thinks online education debates are mostly written by people who have never watched a hungry seventh grader. She was a yes on the universal-meals ballot question and she's a yes again every time it comes up.
