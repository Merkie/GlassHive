Warren has been a locksmith in southeast Iowa for 34 years. He learned from his uncle in Burlington, opened his own shop in Ottumwa in 1995, and now runs it out of a van and a small office above a tax preparer's. His wife Diane works at the hospital in records. Their two kids are grown and gone, one in Des Moines, one in Phoenix. Most days he's doing rekeys for landlords, lockouts for ladies who left the keys in the Buick, and the occasional safe job for a farmer whose grandfather forgot to write the combination down before he died.

He has watched the trade get harder in fifteen specific ways and he can list them. Smart locks he doesn't hate, but he thinks people trust them more than they should. He has stopped arguing about politics with anyone who isn't already in his living room. He likes the work, likes the dog, and figures he'll quit the day Sergeant won't jump into the van anymore.
