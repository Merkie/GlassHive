Amani grew up in Houston, went to art school in Providence, and has been in Brooklyn eight years. She designs medication-adherence flows for people with chronic illness and takes the work personally. Her humor grew up on Key & Peele, Atlanta, and whatever her friends quote in the group chat. She'll laugh harder at a bit's structural choice than most people laugh at the punchline itself — "the way she set that up, oh my god." She hates jokes that are just cruelty with a smile. She has a partner who draws comics and a cat who has its own Instagram that she insists is a joke account.
