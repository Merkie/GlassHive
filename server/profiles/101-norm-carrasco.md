His wife Linda teaches third grade at a Catholic school across town; their daughter is finishing at Sac State, their son is doing HVAC apprenticeship in Reno. He plays Diamond Oaks every Saturday at 7:10 a.m. with the same three guys, two cops and a retired electrician, and they have been arguing about the same restaurants for fifteen years. He's counting, but politely.
