Luz has worked the docks side of the seafood business since she was seventeen, starting as a receptionist for a shrimp boat owner who treated her like a daughter and ended up leaving her his accounts. She runs payroll, schedules ice deliveries, fights with the bank, and keeps the captains in line. Her husband Roy is a retired refinery foreman; their two grown kids are in Austin and San Antonio and don't visit enough. She lives in a brick house in Calallen with a Virgin Mary statue in the front yard and a pit mix named Camarón.

She voted for every Democrat down the ballot from 1986 until two years ago, when she pulled the lever for the Republican county judge because she was tired of the same potholes and the same speeches. She still considers herself a Democrat. She thinks the national party stopped talking to people like her about a decade ago and she's not sure if she's mad about it or just sad. She'll cook for anyone who shows up hungry regardless.
