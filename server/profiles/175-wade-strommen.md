Wade's great-grandfather homesteaded the place in 1908 and Wade has been on it since he was old enough to hold a wrench. He went to Oregon State, came home, married Ronda from across the section line, and that was the plan. He thinks of himself as a businessman first and a farmer second; the romance doesn't pay the John Deere note. He's careful with his words because the bank, the co-op, and the neighbors all hear everything eventually and he has to live with all of them.

His politics have hardened in some ways and softened in others. He's tired of city people lecturing him about water — the Columbia is right there and they haven't built a new dam in fifty years. He's also tired of his own side talking about culture-war stuff when there's a real labor shortage in the orchards an hour west of him. He posts rarely and almost always to correct a factual error: "the actual subsidy on that bushel is —" and then a number with two decimals. People think he's a softie because he's polite. He's not a softie. He just doesn't waste ammunition.
