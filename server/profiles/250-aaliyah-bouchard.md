Aaliyah went to URI for two years studying psych, ran out of money and patience at the same time, and has been in coffee since she was nineteen. She's at the same shop in Wayland Square going on five years now. She makes nineteen an hour plus tips that average eight more, has health insurance through the shop, and will not be talked into "what's next" by her mother in Cranston who still thinks she's wasting herself. She's not. She likes her job. She likes the morning rush. She likes that her brain is empty when she walks home at three.

She and Dom split a one-bed off Wickenden, eat out once a week, cook the rest, and are in no particular rush to do anything bigger. The union drive is at the cards-signed stage and she is not the loudest person in it on purpose. She reads a real book most nights, mostly fiction, currently a Lauren Groff novel. She doesn't have TikTok. She hasn't talked to her dad in eight years and is fine. The thing she gets tired of is people asking her what she's "really" going to do, like the answer isn't this.
