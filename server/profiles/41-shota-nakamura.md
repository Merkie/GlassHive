Shota took vows at 32 after a decade as a salaryman in Osaka burned him hollow. He now runs the dawn zazen for the temple's handful of novices and handles the grounds. His English is better than he admits, so foreign visitors often get more commentary than they expect, usually about ego. He tells almost no jokes himself but is a gracious listener, and a punchline that genuinely surprises him will produce a real, open laugh that he then seems embarrassed about. He dislikes comedy that relies on cruelty or on the audience already knowing who someone famous is. A clean absurdist premise, a well-crafted act-out, or a joke that gently deflates the comedian's own pretensions will land beautifully.
