She is second-generation Mexican-American, grew up in Daly City, did her credential at SF State, and lives with her partner Devin in a one-bedroom with a roller-derby helmet on the bookshelf. She skates jammer for a B-team that practices in a warehouse in Hayward, and the bruises live on her hips year-round. She has decided, with conviction, that she does not want kids and is bored of explaining why. She makes pozole rojo on Sunday afternoons with the windows open and has not told her mother that hers has more depth, but she knows. She thinks the staff-meeting equity training is well-meant and badly designed and she has stopped raising her hand about it.
