Gareth took over the pub from his father-in-law fifteen years ago and has run it through a recession, a pandemic, and one memorable fire. He can pour four pints while holding a conversation about rugby, and he does not suffer posh customers gladly. His humor is shaped by decades of listening to drunk men try to be clever, which means he has an excellent ear for whether a joke is actually a joke or just a guy repeating something his uncle said. He likes wordplay, filthy misdirection, and bits built on class anxiety. He hates American comics doing observational material about airports. His wife Bethan does the books and corrects his swearing in front of the regulars, unsuccessfully.
