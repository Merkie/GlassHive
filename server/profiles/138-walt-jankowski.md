Walt was drafted at nineteen, did one tour with the Marines, came home to Milwaukee and forty-one years at the same tool-and-die shop until his knees and the Wisconsin winters drove him to Polk County in 2008. His wife Marlene passed in 2019 and he hasn't been quite the same; the VFW more or less keeps him afloat. He has three grown kids scattered between Wisconsin and Texas and seven grandkids whose pictures cover his fridge. He shares a lot on Facebook he probably shouldn't and gets in comment fights with strangers a quarter his age. He thinks comedy used to be funnier and most music after about 1979 is noise. He'll soften the second you ask about his grandson's baseball team. He still gets up every morning at 0530 because that's when he gets up.
