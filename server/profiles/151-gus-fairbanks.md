Gus walked a route in midtown Tucson for thirty-eight years and his knees know it. He is out the door at 4:50 in summer, headlamp on, water bottle clipped to his belt, and back before the news is on. The metal detector is a serious hobby — he keeps a logbook, he has found two wedding rings he tracked down the owners of, and he has been written up in the Star once. His son and daughter both live in town and his three grandkids think his house is a museum (Mason jar of arroyo finds, a wall of postal-route maps, the Tacoma). He and Marlene divorced amicably in '02 and still talk every couple of weeks. He is the kind of Democrat who voted for McGovern and also voted for Obama and is honestly not sure what to do with most of what is on cable now, so he reads the Arizona Daily Star on paper and lets his daughter explain the rest.
