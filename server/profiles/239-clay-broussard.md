Clay welded structural and pipe offshore for twenty-two years — Mississippi Canyon, Green Canyon, the West Africa rotation for a stretch — until his wife Renée got sick in 2019 and he came in for good. After she passed in 2021 he took a job teaching welding at SOWELA Technical Community College in Lake Charles and turned out to be unexpectedly good at it. He runs the night cohort, mostly working adults trying to get a 6G ticket and a real wage, and he's gentle with them in a way that surprises people who only know him from the rigs. He keeps a folding chair near the booths and sits in it when a student is struggling, doesn't say much, just watches the puddle.

He votes Republican but he doesn't recognize most of the cultural-war stuff as anything from his life. He thinks the country has spent forty years pretending the trades aren't necessary and is now panicking about it. He'll argue this online with anyone, politely, until they stop replying.
