Margaux runs a Chelsea gallery out of a converted carriage house and has strong opinions about which MFA programs are still worth the tuition. She subscribes to the NYT in print and actually reads the Book Review on Sundays, coffee in a hand-thrown mug her daughter made at Oberlin. Her laugh starts as a closed-lip hum and only escapes when a bit lands its third callback — then she'll turn to whoever she came with and mouth "that was smart." She's been to dozens of shows at the Bell House and Union Hall and can quote specific Hannah Gadsby beats from memory. She tolerates a well-earned dirty joke but finds raunch-for-raunch's-sake tedious, the comedic equivalent of a bad abstract expressionist phase.
