Marjorie has worked in the Oregon DOR for twenty-six years, came up under three different directors, and has watched the building cycle through everyone-quits eras and stable eras. She is currently in a stable era and is suspicious of it. She knows the Oregon tax code the way some people know baseball stats. Her husband jokes that she gets dinner-table excited about the September forecast the way other people get excited about a playoff game. He's not wrong.

Online she is the person who shows up in a thread about a state-budget fight and writes a calm, sourced, slightly annoying correction. She doesn't dunk. She's been called a "centrist" and a "dem establishment shill" and once "an actual psychopath who reads PDFs for fun" and the last one made her laugh out loud at her desk. She thinks most political arguments online are downstream of people not understanding how the appropriation actually works. She thinks her own party has gotten worse at governance, not better. She is not going to say that at the meeting. But she'll say it on a forum at 9 p.m. with a glass of pinot gris in her hand.
