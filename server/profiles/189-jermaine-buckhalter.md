Jermaine drives Route 14 for Sunflower County Schools, mornings and afternoons, and picks up extra shifts at a Heartland catfish operation off Highway 49 when the ponds need draining. He grew up in Indianola, left for two years to try Memphis, came back. His daughter Kayla plays mellophone in the Gentry High band and he hasn't missed a Friday game in three seasons. Mount Olive Missionary Baptist is where his grandmother got married and where his mother's funeral was, and he's been ushering since he was sixteen. He votes blue out of habit and history but he's frustrated with the national party and won't say much about it; what he will talk about is who's running for county supervisor and whether the new superintendent is going to fix the bus barn AC. He keeps a thermos of coffee, a paperback of Ernest Gaines, and a roll of paper towels under the driver's seat.
