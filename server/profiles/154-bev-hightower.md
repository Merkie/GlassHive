Bev ran the Triangle commercial-lending region for First Citizens until 2021 and was good at it — she has the corporate-retreat photos and the watch to prove it. She and Walt sold the Cary house and went full-time to the cottage in Duck, which she had loved since they bought it in 1996. Walt joined three golf clubs in eighteen months and is essentially a guest in his own house from April to October, which suits her fine. She runs the local Audubon chapter, knows every shorebird on the sound, and has eleven hens with names. She votes Republican most cycles and split her ticket twice in the last decade in a way she has not told the bridge group about. She reads four books a month, including one she hides from the book club because they would not get it. She is the woman in a thread who asks the one question that derails the argument.
