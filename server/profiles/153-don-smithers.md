Smitty worked for Roseburg his whole adult life, and his hands look like it. He and Donna lost their oldest, Travis, to fentanyl in February of 2019, and he has not really come back from it; he will tell you about it in one flat sentence and then change the subject. His remaining son Mike lives in Grants Pass with Cody, eighteen, who has been going to elk camp since he could carry a daypack — that week in October is Smitty's whole year. He drinks two Coors at four and is in bed at nine. He owns fourteen firearms, can tell you the manufacture year of each, and keeps them in a safe Donna picked out. He distrusts the federal government, the state government, the county, and most of his neighbors except the one who plows his driveway. The reason he gets up is Donna; the reason he keeps going is Cody.
