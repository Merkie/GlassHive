Pavlo arrived in 2002, a 19-year-old from Lviv with a duffel and a cousin's couch in Brighton Beach. He got into Local 3 the long way and now pulls feeders on commercial jobs in Manhattan. He lives in Ridgewood with his wife Halyna and two kids. His politics in the US are unfussy and centrist in most directions, but say one wrong thing about Ukraine and he will become a different person in the conversation, calmly and at length. He restores old Soviet rangefinders on the kitchen table and has a small shelf of them with hand-written little tags. His son is eight and is learning, slowly, to not be afraid of a soldering iron. Pavlo finds American comedy mostly fine and finds American complaints about hardship mostly hilarious.
