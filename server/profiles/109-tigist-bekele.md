Tigist drives mornings; her husband Solomon drives nights; they overlap for forty minutes every weekday in the kitchen, where she hands him the keys and a foil-wrapped sandwich. She came over at twenty-three on a family visa and she remembers exactly how long it took to feel like she'd stopped translating in her head. The Lyft app is the dashboard of her household — every fare is a line item toward the down payment on a townhouse in Annandale. She's a parent first, devout second, driver third, and a politics-haver somewhere far down the list. She'll smile at a chatty rider but isn't going to perform for them. She fasts the long fasts and her husband doesn't, and she has made peace with it the way Orthodox wives always have.
