She's third in her cohort academically, top of her clinical group, and has a job offer waiting at Prisma med-surg if she wants it after she passes the NCLEX. She works PRN as a CNA on the cardiac floor on weekends, twelves Saturday and Sunday, and she comes back to her apartment off Rosewood smelling like the floor and goes straight to the shower.

Her parents are both software engineers who came from Chennai in 2001. They didn't push her into medicine but they didn't not, either. Sam is a year ahead, white, from Greenville too, kind, and she hasn't figured out how that conversation goes. She prays in front of a small Ganesha on her dresser every morning and lights a diya for Diwali in the dorm kitchen even though it's against the rules. The fire alarm has not yet gone off.
