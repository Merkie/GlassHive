Tyler grew up in Suffolk and started at the shipyard at twenty after a year of community college didn't take. He works on the Virginia-class submarine program in a job that pays well, occasionally insanely well with overtime, and rotates between graveyard and day shifts in a way that's wearing on his marriage in ways he and his wife Brittany are honest about with each other. They have a townhouse in Hampton and they're trying to time a move out toward Suffolk before prices keep climbing. He votes the candidate, not the party, and he genuinely believes the people who think Hampton Roads is a red or blue area haven't actually lived here — "everybody works for the Navy or the shipyard, it's complicated." He is the union guy at the cookout and the deer-camp guy at the union meeting. He is good at both.
