Celeste is the third generation at Marchetti Funeral Home on the West Side. Her grandfather started it in 1953, her father ran it from 1981 to 2018, and she took over as director when he retired. She is licensed for both funeral directing and embalming. Her brother is a dentist in Pittsburgh and was never going to do this. Her two kids are 12 and 9 and she does not know yet if either will want it, and she has decided not to push.

The job is what it is and she's good at it. She has buried a lot of children, more than the average person could carry, and she carries it by going home and being very specifically present with her own. She thinks the cremation rate going from 25% to 65% in her career has changed everything about the business. She thinks every funeral home owner under 50 is quietly worried. She thinks people grieve fine, mostly, when you give them room.
