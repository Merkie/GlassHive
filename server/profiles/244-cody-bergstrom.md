Cody did two years at Bellevue College, hated it, dropped out, did the IBEW aptitude test, scored well, and waited eleven months on the apprenticeship list before he got the call. Now he's pulling wire on a four-story commercial build in Bellevue and going to class on Tuesday and Thursday nights at the JATC. He makes thirty-two an hour and clears a hundred and fifty in OT some weeks and his dad finally stopped giving him advice about money. He lives in a two-bedroom in Kent with another apprentice he met at orientation; rent is fifteen-fifty split and he's saving for a house he knows he can't afford in this market.

He doesn't say much on the jobsite, which the journeymen took as either respect or stupidity until he caught a wiring error that would've cost the GC a week. He likes the work. He likes that when he comes home his hands hurt and the day is done. He doesn't get the white-collar guys he grew up with who post about their mental health on LinkedIn.
