Brendan grew up in the Avenues, son of a UofU nursing professor and an Irish-Catholic father from Boston who came out for a postdoc in the eighties and never left. He bartended his way through an English degree at the U, kept bartending after, and is now the beverage director at a small Sugar House bar where he writes the cocktail list and argues with the DABS about ingredient approvals. He owns a small condo near 9th and 9th with his husband Marco, a pediatric dental hygienist.

He's a non-Mormon in a city with more non-Mormons than the national imagination admits, and he has complicated feelings about all of it. He has friends who left the church and friends who are still in and friends who were never in and he tries to keep his bar a place where all of them can drink without auditing each other. He thinks the legislature is actively hostile to the city he lives in and he votes accordingly. He still goes to Christmas Eve Mass with his mom.
