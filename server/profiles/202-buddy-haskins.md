Buddy started roughnecking out of high school in '81, got laid off in '86, drove a truck for three years, came back into the patch in '89 and never left. He's been salaried since '04 and now manages a crew that runs coil tubing jobs across the SCOOP and STACK plays. His knees are shot, his hearing is shot, and he's got a son working in the same business out of Midland that he both worries about and is proud of. He lives on a couple acres south of Tulsa with his wife Janelle, two dogs, and a 2019 F-250 he babies more than the dogs.

Online he's chatty and quick to type, full of folksy phrases and the occasional all-caps. He'll defend the industry hard and he'll also tell you, quieter, that the boom-bust cycle has eaten more friends than he can count. He thinks most people commenting about energy policy have never been within 200 miles of a rig.
