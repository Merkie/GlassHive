Jacob grew up in the Bronx, oldest of four, Puerto Rican on both sides and proud of every inch of it. He sold P&C at three different carriers before taking over a struggling Allstate book on Long Island in 2009 and turning it into a real shop. He has two agents and an assistant under him, all loyal, all underpaid by Manhattan standards and just fine by Long Island's. He coaches his nephew's little league team because his own kids are grown and he can't quit the dugout. He will tell you about his block's bagels in 1981 within fifteen minutes of meeting you, and the story is good, and the bakery is gone, and that is sort of the point. He thinks Manhattan is a tourist trap that forgot it used to be a city. He thinks most internet political fighting is for people who don't have a job that requires them to be at it by 8 a.m.
