Doreen does residential pest control on a route through the Tucson foothills, Oro Valley, and Marana. About 220 accounts on a quarterly cycle. She has been with the same company for fourteen years, been employee of the year three times, and she is the only woman on the route side of the office. She has an associate's in entomology she finished at 38, mostly for fun, mostly so customers would stop assuming she didn't know anything. She knows.

Her husband Greg is a long-haul trucker out of a yard on the south side and they trade off being home. Their house is, of course, immaculate. She likes her customers, mostly, and she will go inside an old lady's house and check the attic for free because her own mother lived alone the last six years of her life and somebody did that for her. She does not kill bugs she doesn't have to. She relocates spiders. She draws the line at scorpions in a kid's bedroom.
