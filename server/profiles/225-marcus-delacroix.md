Marcus has been driving for SEPTA for nineteen years, the last eleven on the 23, which used to run Chestnut Hill to South Philly until they cut it in half. He still calls the whole stretch "the 23" out of habit. He lives in West Oak Lane in the rowhouse his mother left him, with his wife Tanika who works in medical billing and a 16-year-old daughter who runs the 400. He's seen three fights, two births, a heart attack, and one guy try to pay with a live chicken. He has opinions about every single intersection in this city and which signal cycles are stupid.

The union is the part he respects without irony. He went to two strikes and a lockout and he still wears the 234 windbreaker on cold mornings. He thinks the people who design bus schedules have never ridden a bus. He likes his job most of the time, hates it some of the time, and would not let his daughter do it for any amount of money.
