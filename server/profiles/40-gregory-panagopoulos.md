Greg's parents emigrated from Thessaloniki in the seventies and he has been running his practice in the Eastern Suburbs since 2004. His patients think he is a lovely, gentle man with a calm chair-side manner, which he is. What they do not know is that in the staff room he and his hygienist Maree have a running bit about a specific patient's overbite that is frankly unrepeatable. His humor was forged at a Sydney medical school in the nineties and has not mellowed. He loves a rude Aussie premise delivered with commitment. He cannot stand wholesome American sitcom beats. When a joke genuinely lands he laughs a full body laugh that shakes the overhead light and has, on one occasion, scared a child.
