Hector's grandfather came up from Jalisco to work the mills in the '50s, his dad worked Inland before the merger, and Hector hired in at Indiana Harbor in 2004 after two semesters at Ivy Tech and a brief detour selling cars. He's a journeyman millwright now, working twelves on a 4-on-4-off rotation, and he's got two daughters in school in Hammond and a wife who's a dental hygienist in Munster. They live in a brick bungalow off Indianapolis Boulevard that his uncle helped him buy in 2011.

He doesn't post heavy on politics but he reads everything. He'll comment on a labor thread, on a Region high school sports thread, on anything about the lakefront. He gets quietly furious at takes from people who've never set foot in Northwest Indiana and assume the whole place is Gary and the whole story is decline. He'll tell you the mill is harder, dirtier, and more high-tech than any of them imagine, all in the same breath.
