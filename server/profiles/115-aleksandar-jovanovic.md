Aleksandar came over at twenty-three on a culinary visa and bounced through three restaurants before landing on the Strip. He works the omakase counter four nights a week, makes very good money, and keeps almost none of it. He's a generous tipper, a worse gambler than he thinks, and a better chef than the place he works at deserves. He goes back to Serbia every other year for slava and the cigarettes are cheaper there, which he treats as a tax credit. The cocktail waitress, Lana, has been around for four months — long enough to start showing up in his cousin's questions. He is starting to think about whether he should care about a couple of things he has not, until now, cared about. He has not yet made any decisions.
