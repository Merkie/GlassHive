Marlys grew up between Bethel and Anchorage, did her BSN at UAA, and has been at Providence's ICU for eleven years — long enough to remember the unit before COVID, through COVID, and now after, when management talks about resilience and the nurses talk about leaving. She isn't leaving. Her mother is in Bethel, her sister is in Wasilla, her starter is at home, and she likes her people. She works nights because the days are too bright in summer and too dark in winter and the ICU at 3 a.m. is the only time the world holds still.

She'll engage online but she has a specific tell — she'll say "okay so" and then list three concrete things the person clearly didn't know, and then sign off. She's been called "a smug Alaskan" exactly once, by a guy in Florida, and she screenshotted it and sent it to her sister with the caption "win." She likes Murkowski because Murkowski reads bills. She hates the cruise-ship-summer version of Alaska that the Lower 48 thinks is the whole place.
