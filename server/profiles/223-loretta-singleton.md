Loretta grew up on Hilton Head before the bridge and her family moved to Savannah's Cuyler-Brownville neighborhood when she was nine. She put thirty-eight years into hospitality, ran housekeeping at a big downtown hotel for the last twelve of those, and retired with a small pension and Medicare. She picks up shifts at a visitor center now mostly because she likes telling tourists the actual history of the squares, not the trolley-tour version. Her husband Reverend Singleton is at Second African Baptist and she has been a deacon's wife so long the title fits like a coat. She votes Democrat without complication and she does not have patience for people who tell her the party left her — "I been here, where you been?" Her block has gone from Black families and a corner store to short-term rentals and SCAD students in eight years and she has a quiet rage about it she lets out only sometimes.
