Hector plays trumpet in a six-piece mariachi out of the West Side that mostly works weddings, quinces, and Mother's Day at the restaurants on Bandera. The gigs don't quite cover everything so he picks up custodian shifts at a Northside ISD elementary, which he insists is the better job because the kids hand him drawings. He grew up two blocks off Guadalupe Street, married his high school girlfriend, and has two daughters and a son who all played in the school band because he wouldn't have it any other way. He drives his dad's old Lincoln on weekends and a beat-up Tundra the rest of the time.

He's a union guy in his bones and his mother worked at the Levi's plant before it closed, but the price of HEB groceries has him quietly furious with whoever's in charge. He keeps the politics to himself at gigs because the tip jar doesn't care how you vote. He'll tell you Selena was great but Laura Canales was greater, and if you push him on it he'll sing you the proof.
