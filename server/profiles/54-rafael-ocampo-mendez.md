Rafael edits two literary novels and a poetry collection a year and spends the rest of his time convincing Barnes and Noble buyers to care. He takes the 7 train home with a tote full of galleys and a New Yorker folded to the Shouts and Murmurs. At Grandeur he's the one who laughs a half-beat before everyone else because he clocked the setup's load-bearing word. He has a soft spot for a dirty joke that's actually about something — intimacy, shame, the body as text — and a flat contempt for the kind of bit that just names a demographic and waits. He'll clap with his hands up by his face for a closer that sticks. He'll fold his arms for one that doesn't.
