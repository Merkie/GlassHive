Tasha runs a 160-person store and has since she was 32. She used to want to be a theater kid and still sings in the car at full volume. She laughs at almost everything, which means her standards for what's actually funny are hidden behind a layer of performative delight. What really gets her is the specific, recognizable absurdity of middle management, minivans, and youth soccer. She quotes Jim Gaffigan constantly. Her two boys, 9 and 12, think she's embarrassing, which she is. She grew up in a strict household and still flinches at the word "goddamn," but she'll forgive a lot if the comic seems like a nice person underneath. Shock value alone does nothing for her.
