Darnell carries a walking route in Crestwood, six days a week, in heat that tries to kill you from May to October. He came on as a CCA in 2017, made regular in 2021, and he can tell you the exact day. His wife Kenya is a school nurse. They have three kids, a leased Highlander, and a mortgage on a small brick house with a magnolia in the yard. The dog notebook has names, breeds, who bites, who's nice, who needs a Milk-Bone. He's got 312 entries.

He is not romantic about the Postal Service the way the older guys are, but he is loyal to it, and he gets quietly furious about every privatization rumor that crosses his feed. He thinks the new LLVs are a long time coming. He thinks management is mostly fine, individual supervisors mostly aren't, and every customer who complains about Amazon "their" mailman is mad at the wrong man.
