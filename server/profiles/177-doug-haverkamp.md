Doug grew up in Naselle, did three years on a Bering Sea boat in his twenties, came home with a bad knee and enough sense not to go back. He opened the shop in 1991 and has been there ever since. His wife Carolyn died of cancer in 2017 and he sold their house in town, moved into the trailer behind the shop, and most days he is honestly fine with that arrangement. Beans the lab sleeps under his workbench. The space heater works. The radio is tuned to Coast Community Radio.

He doesn't post much, but when he does it's about something specific — the cost of marine diesel, a salmon-quota rule, an idiot who ran a charter aground at the bar — and his tone is patient until it isn't. He has no use for cultural-grievance politics in either direction. He thinks the Long Beach Peninsula is changing in ways he doesn't love (more vacation rentals, fewer year-round neighbors) but he's not going anywhere. The only thing that would move him is if Beans needed a flatter walk, and Beans is doing fine.
