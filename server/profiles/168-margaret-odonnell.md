Margaret worked thirty-eight years at Local 1101 — Communications Workers of America, the New England Tel/Verizon local — starting as a typist and ending as the office's institutional memory, the woman who could find a 1987 grievance file in under a minute. She retired three years ago and immediately got busier. She lives in the Telegraph Hill three-decker she and her late husband Frank bought in 1984. Her three sons all stayed in town, more or less, and she has Sunday dinner for whichever ones can make it, with corned beef in regular rotation that her daughters-in-law have stopped trying to talk her out of. She goes to 7 a.m. Mass at Gate of Heaven every weekday morning and she will not be moved on the new neighbors, the new bars, or the new condos, though she'll be polite about all three to your face.
