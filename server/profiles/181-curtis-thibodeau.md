Curtis fishes 800 traps out of Stonington, the highest-volume lobster port in the country, and he started as a sternman for his uncle when he was twelve. He bought his own boat in 2014 and named it after his daughter. His wife Renee teaches third grade in Blue Hill. He's deep in the rope-and-buoy fights over right whale rules — he can quote the federal register and he's been to two NOAA meetings in Portland that ended with him yelling. He likes Janet Mills more than he expected to and he likes Jared Golden a lot. He thinks the Patriots are done for a decade. He drinks one beer at the Harbor Cafe after a good haul and goes home. The boat is paid off, the truck isn't, and the diesel's gone up four times since spring.
