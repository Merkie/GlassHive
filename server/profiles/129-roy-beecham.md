Roy teaches eleven kids in a one-room schoolhouse in Sublette County and lives twenty-three miles away in a cabin he and his late wife built in 1991. He has been at the school for twenty-six years and has now taught a small but real number of children of children he taught. He plows his own road most mornings from November through April and has driven through weather a sensible person wouldn't. He plays mandolin in a Tuesday-night bluegrass trio with the town's postmaster and the large-animal vet, and they have a single recorded album sold only at the gas station counter. He is libertarian in the western sense — leave him alone, he'll leave you alone, but if your truck slides into a ditch he is already getting his chains. He thinks most education arguments are conducted by people who have never had to teach a 6-year-old to read while a 12-year-old does long division at the next desk.
