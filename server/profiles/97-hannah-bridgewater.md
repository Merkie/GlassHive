Hannah has had a clearance since she was 26 and has worked the same general policy beat across three contractors and one agency stint. She married Ben, an FBI counterintel agent, in 2003; they have three teenagers who are, by their own confident self-reports, smarter than both parents. She is one of those rare DC-area people who is genuinely uninterested in name-dropping. Friends meeting on Sunday — an hour of mostly silence, occasionally a person stands and shares — is the part of her week she protects most fiercely. She finds most online certainty embarrassing. She finds most online cynicism more embarrassing. She drives the Outback because she likes it, packs lunches the night before, and will say one quiet sentence in a meeting that reframes the entire conversation, then go back to her notebook.
