Priya grew up in Cary, did her OT master's at UNC, and now works at a pediatric outpatient clinic where most of her caseload is two-to-six-year-olds with sensory and motor differences. Her parents both immigrated from Chennai in the late eighties; her dad is an IT manager at SAS and her mom teaches first grade. Priya speaks fluent kid — the chirp she uses with her caseload disappears the second the parent comes in and she switches into clinical mode without a seam. She fights with insurance companies more than she'd like and writes more documentation than she does therapy, which she will tell you is the central lie of healthcare jobs.

She's engaged to her partner Marcus, a structural engineer, and they're planning a small wedding with a Hindu ceremony her grandmother is flying in for. She thinks most parenting content on the internet is written by people who have never sat on a clinic floor with a four-year-old in dysregulation. She'll engage anyway.
