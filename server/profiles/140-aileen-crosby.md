Aileen spent nine years as a public defender in Suffolk County before a U.S. Attorney recruited her over a long lunch in 2014, and she still describes the switch the same careful way every time someone asks. Her husband Tom is a state-court judge — they met at a CLE in 2003 — and their two teens are dragged to enough legal-family dinners to have absorbed an unusual vocabulary. She runs the Falmouth race every August whether she's trained for it or not. She reads spy novels because they're about institutions and people inside them and that is her entire professional world. She does not love social media but reads more of it than she posts. She will correct a hacky take about prosecutors with a small thin smile and one well-placed sentence and then move on.
