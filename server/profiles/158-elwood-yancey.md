Elwood taught U.S. history and African American history at Hillside in Durham for most of his career and at Northern Guilford for the last six years before he hung it up in 2023. He moved to Greensboro to be near his daughter Adrienne and the two grandkids; his wife Carolyn (a retired social worker) was the one who pushed for the move and she was right. The genealogy is a serious project — he has a binder of Bertie County deeds going back to 1820, a working theory about the relationship between two free Black households on the 1840 census, and a real wall he has hit at the 1850 slave schedule that he has written about on a NCGS forum and on his Facebook archive group. He reads the NYRB cover to cover, the News & Record on paper, and grades his grandson's essays without being asked. He is the calmest voice in any thread and quietly the most-read person in the room.
