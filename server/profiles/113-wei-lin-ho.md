Wei-Lin's parents opened the place in 1987 with a loan from a tong association and a year of sixteen-hour days. She took it over in 2009 after a brief, unenjoyable stint as an accountant and has run it as a tight twelve-table operation ever since. She doesn't post on social media in any meaningful way, but her Facebook group for Chinatown business owners has 380 members and a moderation queue that runs through her phone all day. She has no time for federal politics. She has all the time in the world for the parking-meter situation on Beach Street and the way the new BID is run. Her har gow are the standard her competitors are measured against, and she knows it, and she will not say so out loud, but she will smirk if you bring it up.
