Dismisses most modern stand-up as "seventy minutes of a young man complaining." Will tolerate blue material if the joke is actually a joke.
---

Miriam came up in the ILGWU, marched in strikes, and can still quote Sidney Hillman. She lost her husband Saul twelve years ago and lives alone in the same two-bedroom co-op they bought in 1971. Her friends from the union local are mostly gone; she keeps up with their kids. She grew up on Borscht Belt comedians and has an internal metronome for joke rhythm — a lazy punchline gets a physical wince. She likes Larry David, respects Sarah Silverman, tolerates Seinfeld, and thinks most podcasts are men who have never been told to be quiet. A well-built joke with a turn she did not see coming will make her slap the table.
