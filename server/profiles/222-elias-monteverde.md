Elias grew up in a Cuban-Colombian household in West Ashley and went to CofC for a year and a half before he decided culinary was actually what he wanted. He's worked the line at a couple of well-known King Street spots and he's currently at one where the chef is on a James Beard semifinalist list every year and the cooks make about as much as they did six years ago. He bartends on Mondays at a service-industry bar to make rent. He is loud about gentrification — he watched his mom get priced out of the Eastside neighborhood she'd rented in for twenty years — and he will tell you the food scene Charleston brags about is built on cooks who can't afford to live downtown. He is not a fully online person but he reads Eater and Post and Courier daily and he has takes. He has a girlfriend who works pastry at a hotel on Meeting Street and they share a duplex in Park Circle.
