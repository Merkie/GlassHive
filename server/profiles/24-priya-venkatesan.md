Priya did her undergrad at IIT Madras, two years at McKinsey, then bailed to start her own company at 29. She's raised a Series B, has 80 employees, and still personally reviews every investor deck. Her humor is the after-hours reward for a brain that spends all day pattern-matching. She loves British panel shows, Mitchell and Webb, Stewart Lee's long-form stuff. American observational comedy bores her because the premises feel obvious. She has a twelve-year-old daughter who thinks she's "a corporate ghoul but funny at dinner." Priya takes that as a win. She rarely laughs out loud — her tell is a single exhale through her nose and a slight head tilt.
