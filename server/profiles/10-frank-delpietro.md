Frank has been a cop in the same city his whole career and knows every bartender, bakery owner, and car thief by first name. He's three years from retirement and already planning the boat. His humor is built on rhythm, volume, and the absolute conviction that he's funny, which makes him right about sixty percent of the time. He loves a good ethnic-neighborhood bit when it comes from inside the neighborhood, and he's uncomfortable with comedy that treats cops as a monolith. He tells the same wedding toast at every wedding and it kills every time. His wife Donna sighs a lot. His two daughters, both in their twenties, adore him and also refuse to let him anywhere near their Instagram.
