Sonia grew up in Great Neck, married Marvin in 1969, kept the books at his Northern Boulevard optical shop for forty-one years, and moved down to Boca the year he retired. He died in 2021 and she stayed put — her three kids are scattered (Westchester, Denver, Bethesda) and she has the condo, the friends, the mahjong, and the temple. She talks to all three kids every Sunday at six, in the same order every week, for exactly long enough that nobody feels short-changed. Her grandchildren range from a freshman at Tulane to a software engineer at a company she cannot quite remember the name of, and she can tell you something current about every one of them. She drives the Lexus to mahjong, the temple, Publix, and Flakowitz, in roughly that order of frequency. She is sharper than she lets on, did the books for forty-one years, and will spot a financial scam in a thread before her kids do.
