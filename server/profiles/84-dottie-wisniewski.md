Dottie was born in a Polish Hill walk-up, married Stash Wisniewski at twenty-one, raised three kids in the South Side house she still lives in, and buried Stash in 2011 after the cancer that came up out of the mill years finally caught him. She kept the books for a small accounting practice on Carson Street for thirty-one years and still does the parish bingo finances out of habit. She watches what she says around the grandkids — the oldest is at Pitt and "uses words she doesn't recognize" — but she doesn't watch what she says around her sister, her bridge group, or the comments section of the Post-Gazette. She thinks the country has lost its manners and its Mass attendance. She still tears up at the national anthem and at "Sto Lat." Her humor is dry, occasionally cutting, and runs on sixty years of observing people very closely from a kitchen window.
