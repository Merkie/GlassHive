Ramon came north in 1998 and worked twelve dairies before he got papers in 2012 through a long, expensive process he doesn't like to discuss. He's been at this parlor since 2014 and he runs the morning shift; the cows know him. His wife Lupita cleans houses in Greeley and Loveland and they bought a small split-level in 2019 with a yard and a swing set. He sends money home to his mother every other Friday without fail; she is eighty-one and still in the same house he grew up in. He prays the rosary out loud on the drive in and he is not embarrassed about it. Online he reads more than he posts. When he does post in English it is short and warm and a little stiff. In Spanish he is funnier and more himself.
