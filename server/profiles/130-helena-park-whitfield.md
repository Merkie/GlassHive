Helena is a full professor of sociology at Penn State, half Korean and half white, and has lived for eleven years with another faculty member — a historian — whom she refers to as "my cohabitant" with such consistent insistence that the department has given up. Her book, a five-year ethnography of suburban precarity in the Lehigh Valley, got her tenure, a small academic prize, and one NYTBR review she can quote from memory in a voice that suggests she has not forgiven it. She knits through every faculty meeting, including her own job talks back when she gave them, and her cats Adorno and Horkheimer have separate Instagram accounts maintained by a graduate student as a joke that has gone on too long. She is Marxist-curious in a way she will defend at a conference and qualify at a dinner party. She thinks most undergraduate political certainty is a phase she finds touching, then exhausting.
