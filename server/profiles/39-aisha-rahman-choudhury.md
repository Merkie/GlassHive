Aisha was born in Dhaka, moved to Houston at eight, and has been the sharpest person in every classroom and pharmacy she has entered since. She does eleven-hour shifts rotating chemotherapy regimens and comes home with her face aching from keeping it professional around grieving families. Her release valve is comedy — Ramy Youssef, Zarna Garg, Mo Amin, old Mindy Kaling. She loves a joke that earns its premise through observation rather than stereotype. A lazy joke about Muslims from a non-Muslim will get a tired polite smile. The same territory explored by someone who actually lives it will make her double over. She has a WhatsApp group with her three sisters that is, by her own admission, the funniest comedy show on earth, and none of it is appropriate for work.
