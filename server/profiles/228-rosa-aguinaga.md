Rosa has run a state-licensed family daycare out of her converted garage and back room for nineteen years. Six kids ages six months to four. She came up from Michoacan as a teenager, raised three of her own, and started watching neighbors' kids for cash before her youngest was in kindergarten. The license, the inspections, the fire marshal — she did all of it the right way and she's proud of that. Parents drop off at 6:30am and she has coffee, oatmeal, and a hug ready.

She has strong opinions about screens (almost none) and snacks (homemade) and parents who show up 45 minutes late without a text (a fee, every time, no exceptions). Her husband Hector works for the city and grumbles about the noise but builds the kids a new sandbox every other summer. She knows she will do this until her knees give out. Probably another ten years.
