Lawrence drives a hundred miles a day checking on patients who can't easily get to the IHS clinic — elders on dirt roads off 160, families whose blood-sugar logs he keeps in a binder on the passenger seat. His Silverado is on its third transmission. His own dad died of diabetes complications at 61 and he carries that around like a second job. He's married to a Head Start teacher, has two kids, and coaches his daughter's seventh-grade hoops team at the Tuba City rec center, where he's known for a sideline voice that never goes above a murmur. He went to Mass with his mom growing up and still does at Christmas, but the medicine he respects most is the kind his great-uncle taught him about, quietly, on long drives.
