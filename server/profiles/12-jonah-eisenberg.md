Jonah retired from Yale three years ago and has not stopped going to the department. He wrote four books on Reconstruction, has a corner office he refuses to surrender, and grades his grandchildren's college essays without being asked. His wife Miriam died in 2019; he still sets the Shabbat table for two out of habit. He collects New Yorker cartoon anthologies and can date any Roz Chast by decade. He thinks comedians today mistake volume for wit and will say so at dinner parties. He does, privately, find Larry David upsetting in a way he enjoys. He tips waiters generously, corrects their grammar quietly, and believes a well-placed subordinate clause can still save a joke from mediocrity.
