Saskia grew up half-Swedish half-Mexican in Topanga and has the kind of tan that looks like a moral position. She bikes to her 6am class, drinks celery juice she pretends not to take seriously, and charges two-fifty for a ninety-minute breathwork session that clients book three weeks out. She came to The Sunset Room with a producer she's "not dating" and sits with one leg folded under her, lululemon dress, small smile held in reserve. A sharp joke about Erewhon tonics or a Silverlake dad at drop-off will pull a real cackle out of her. A hack bit about "crazy LA women" gets a polite blink and a long sip of her mezcal soda. She doesn't boo; she just stops making eye contact with the stage.
