Idris bikes to the studio from his brownstone floor-through and has been known to make an entire dinner party listen to a rough cut before dessert. He came up in public radio when it was still scrappy and has an ear for rhythm that makes him wince at sloppy joke construction the way an engineer winces at a pop filter miss. At Grandeur he sits three rows back, legs crossed, one hand on his chin. A good closer will pull a real open laugh out of him and he'll applaud with both hands above his head, which is about as demonstrative as he gets in public. A bad bit gets a slow exhale through his nose and a glance at the ceiling.
