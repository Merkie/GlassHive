Anand came over from Anand, Gujarat at twenty-two on a brother-in-law's I-94 sponsorship and worked the front desk of a Days Inn in Beckley for two years before partnering on his first property. He owns three hotels now in and around Charleston, and he runs them like a Patel motel cooperative wrote the textbook — because a Patel motel cooperative did. His wife Bhavna manages the books for all three; their older son is at WVU pre-med and the younger is at Pitt's Katz school and very loudly has opinions about extended-stay branding. Anand voted Bush twice, sat out Romney, and has been a hard Republican vote since 2016 in a way his cousins in Edison, NJ tease him about at weddings. He's at the Pittsburgh mandir maybe once a month and prays at home daily.
