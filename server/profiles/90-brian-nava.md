Brian's grandfather came up from Jalisco in the sixties, his dad ran a body shop in Cicero, and Brian apprenticed into Local 130 at twenty-one because his uncle pulled the string. He's eight years in, a journeyman now, and on track to be a foreman in a couple years if he doesn't blow it. He lifts four days a week, rides the Bonneville from April to October and complains about Chicago's potholes the entire time, and dates sporadically and inconveniently. He likes the union, hates the politics around it, and thinks most online debate about labor is people who have never filed a grievance. His humor is the trades-floor humor of a guy who has been roasted for eight hours straight and learned to roast back faster. He'll defend the union, defend his guys, defend his bike, and pick fights about the Cubs bullpen until Mod tools come out.
