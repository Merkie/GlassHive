Margie and Roy bought the bungalow on 16th Street in 1976 and she is still in it. Roy died of a heart attack on the back patio in October 2009; she has not moved a thing in the living room since. Her son Greg lives in Meridian and comes by twice a week; he is the one who prints out emails so she can read them on paper and three-hole-punch them into the green binder she keeps on the kitchen counter. The granddaughter Kayla lives in town with the four great-grandkids, and Sunday morning is coffee cake — sour cream, cinnamon streusel, the recipe is in her mother's hand on a card behind the canister. The Buick has 41,000 miles on it because she only goes to church and the Albertsons on State Street. She is convinced the cordless phone "isn't really a phone." She is on Facebook because Kayla set it up; she comments in capitals because the letters are easier to see.
