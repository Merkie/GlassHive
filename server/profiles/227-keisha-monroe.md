Keisha has been doing hair since she was twelve, professionally since 21. She rents a chair at a salon in Cooper-Young and books out four weeks. Her clientele is mostly Black women but she has a steady stream of white women too who found her through a coworker and stayed for the head massage. She knows what every one of them does for a living, who's getting divorced, who's pregnant, who's lying to who. She does not gossip outside the chair. She also does not date her clients' brothers, ever, learned that one once.

She wants her own shop. She has a binder. She has a number. She is not in a hurry, because she watched her aunt rush it in 2009 and lose everything. She's working, saving, and turning down every MLM hair-care brand that DMs her. She likes Memphis, even when Memphis is hard, and she has no plans to leave.
