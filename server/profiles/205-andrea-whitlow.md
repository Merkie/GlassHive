Andrea grew up on the east side, her mom a DPS teacher and her dad a city water department lifer. She did Cass Tech, then U of M for architecture, then a stretch in Chicago before coming home in 2017 because it was time. She owns a 1920s brick on a tree-lined block in West Village that she's slowly, carefully restoring. She runs projects ranging from school renovations to mixed-use developments and she's the person at her firm who actually pushes back when a developer tries to cheap out on a community-facing facade.

Online she's measured but not soft. She has zero patience for ruin-porn coverage of Detroit or for the recurring genre of "Detroit is back!" think-pieces written by people who've spent four hours downtown. She'll engage thoughtfully on housing, schools, transit, and she'll name names when a politician deserves it. She's seen too much to be optimistic and too much to be cynical either.
