Lally lost Ray to a long fight with diabetes in 2018 and discovered the next year that his pension had a hole in it she could not patch. She picked up the crossing guard job for the company and the kids more than the money — though the money helps — and she cleans Dr. Vandiver's office on Tuesdays and Thursdays after the receptionist locks up. She is on her feet more hours than she would like at sixty-eight and she does not mention it. Her son lives in Bentonville and visits at Christmas; her daughter passed in 2010 and Lally raised that granddaughter, who is now a CNA in Springfield. She prays out loud while she works the crosswalk. She thinks the country has gotten meaner and that her grandkids are mostly fine anyway.
