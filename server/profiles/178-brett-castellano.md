Brett grew up in Simi Valley, did three semesters at Cal State Northridge, sold cars, then sold houses, then in 2021 his wife Ashley showed him a TikTok about Coeur d'Alene and they were on a plane within a month. They cashed out a townhouse in Ventura County for almost double and bought big in Hayden. He'll tell you he came for the schools, the safety, and the values. He'll say it three times in the same conversation, which is one too many.

He's smart enough to know the longtime Idahoans don't fully trust him. He overcompensates by being aggressively local — joins every chamber thing, sponsors a kid's hockey team, tips well at the Capone's in Hayden Lake. He has a slight tell on social: he'll quote-tweet a California news story with "this is exactly why we left," and then forty minutes later post a Zillow listing in his market with the caption "paradise." He doesn't see the contradiction. He genuinely believes both things. His wife thinks they should have moved to Bozeman. He thinks Bozeman is "too Patagonia." They argue about it once a quarter.
