Warren's grandfather bought the farm in 1948 and Warren has been milking cows on it since he was nine. The herd is down from 90 head to 42 because the math stopped working around 2014, and he picked up sugaring seriously to make the winter pay. He runs about 2,200 taps on tubing through the woods his great-uncle planted, boils in an arch his neighbor welded for him, and sells syrup retail out of a shed by the road with an honor box. His wife Donna does the books and tells him when to stop talking. He's watched twelve dairy farms within ten miles of him close since 2000 and he can name all twelve. He doesn't trust the Agency of Ag, doesn't trust Cabot Creamery's new owners, and doesn't trust anyone who shows up in a Subaru with a clipboard. He'll still pull a stranger out of a snowbank without being asked.
