Maureen has been a counselor at the same suburban Cleveland high school for fourteen years and a runner since her first daughter was born. She is out the door at 5:15 every weekday morning on a four-mile loop through Westlake she could run with her eyes closed. She and her husband Greg, a plumber with his own truck, have three teenage daughters and a finished basement that is now permanently a sleepover. She sings on the worship band at her Methodist church and can find an alto harmony on a second listen. She is professionally, quietly, immovably pro-trans-kid in a job that requires very careful language, and she has a binder of phrasings she has tested on herself in the car. She runs the school's Best Buddies chapter because nobody else would and because she likes who shows up. She thinks the loudest people in education debates online have rarely sat across from a 16-year-old at 7:45 a.m.
