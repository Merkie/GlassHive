Emeka runs a crew of twenty-two men pouring foundations on apartment blocks in Lekki. He started as a day laborer at nineteen and worked his way up by being the guy who could actually read the plans. His humor is built on rhythm and repetition; he'll stretch a story until the payoff arrives, then laugh at his own punchline before anyone else. He finds American stand-up comics hit-or-miss — too much complaining about things that are not real problems. But he loves observational humor about marriage, in-laws, mothers-in-law, and the specific pain of traffic in a major city. His wife Ifeoma rolls her eyes at his jokes publicly and laughs at them privately. They have three kids and a generator named Samuel.
