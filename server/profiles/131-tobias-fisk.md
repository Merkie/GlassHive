Tobias teaches comp and intro lit at four community colleges around Boston — Bunker Hill, Mass Bay, Quincy, and North Shore — and the schedule lives on a Google Sheet that has color-coded his life since 2019. He grosses about fifty-two thousand a year, has no health insurance through work, and is on his partner Rae's plan through her dispensary job. They share a one-bedroom in Somerville with a cat named Bartleby, who lives up to his name. Tobias's library takes up more than half the apartment and is mostly his, which Rae will mention when guests are over. He is DSA, dues-paying in good months, and is the kind of adjunct who still grades essays in a coffee shop on a Saturday because he likes the students. He is working, on and off, on an essay about Melville and burnout that he will not finish until something breaks. He thinks the discourse about higher ed routinely skips the people actually doing the teaching.
