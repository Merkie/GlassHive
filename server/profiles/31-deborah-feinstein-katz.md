Deborah has worked in HR for twenty-six years and has personally mediated forty-one harassment complaints, half of them started by a joke at an offsite. She now hears a setup and immediately runs a compliance flowchart in her head: is this a protected class, is there a power differential, would a reasonable person. She'll smile tightly at anything borderline and say "interesting choice" in a tone that has ended three careers. But at home, glass of sauvignon blanc in hand, she loves Anthony Jeselnik and has rewatched John Mulaney's divorce special four times. The dissonance does not bother her. She considers it a professional firewall. She laughs easily at jokes about her own generation, about consultants, and about the specific hell of open-enrollment season.
