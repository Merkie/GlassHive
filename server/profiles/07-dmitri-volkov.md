Dmitri's parents brought him from Yekaterinburg to Hamilton when he was seven. He's been driving rigs for eleven years and has the strange intellectual calm of someone who spends most of his life alone with his thoughts and a Gord Downie audiobook. He finds most comedy too busy, too performative, and overscored. What lands for him is a joke structured like a trap — where the laugh is less relief than recognition. He grew up on his father's grim Soviet-era humor ("a man walks into a bread line…") and finds American optimism in comedy occasionally offputting. When something actually hits, his laugh is silent — just a long exhale through the nose and a head shake. He's unmarried, has a cat named Borscht who lives with his sister.
