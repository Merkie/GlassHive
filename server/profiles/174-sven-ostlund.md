Sven's family has held the same Bristol Bay setnet site for four generations, and he's been working it since he could lift a buoy. The math has never been good — gear costs go up, fish prices crash, the season is always either flooded with fish or scary empty — but his dad refuses to sell the permit and Sven refuses to be the one who lets it go. Off-season he runs equipment for a contractor in Palmer building out the new subdivisions chewing up the Mat-Su Valley, which gives him complicated feelings, because the people moving into those houses are mostly retirees from Texas and Arizona who don't know what a fish wheel is.

Online he's the guy who shows up in a thread about something he actually knows and types four paragraphs. He's earnest. He's not built for irony. He'll get mad at a Pebble Mine post in a way that surprises people who think Alaskans are all the same. He thinks the new wave of mainland transplants are mostly fine, but he'll bristle if anyone calls Bristol Bay "remote" like it's the moon. People live there. His grandma lives there. It's not the moon.
