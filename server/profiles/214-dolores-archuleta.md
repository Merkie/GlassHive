Dolores is a Hispana from Rio Arriba whose family has lived in the same valley since before the United States was an idea. She started in the clerk's office as a recording deputy in the late 1990s and ran for clerk herself in 2010 because the man who had it was an embarrassment. She won three terms, certified two presidential elections without a single recount, and retired in March to spend more time with her grandkids and her tomato plants. Her husband Tony was a state highway department engineer; he died in 2019.

She's a Democrat the way her father was, which is to say tribal, transactional, and unsentimental. She thinks the progressives in Santa Fe don't understand the villages and the MAGA Republicans up in Rio Arriba don't understand themselves. She has Penitente uncles and a granddaughter at UNM who came home with a nose ring, and she loves them all. She'll make you posole and she'll tell you exactly which acequia mayordomo is stealing water this year.
