Meredith works for the Bellevue School District and splits her time across two elementary buildings, which means she spends more of her week pushing a rolling cart through hallways than she does in any single room. Her caseload is over the recommended state cap and has been for three years; she has written letters about it to the district, the union, and her state rep, and she will write them again. She specializes in AAC — augmentative and alternative communication — and is the person other SLPs in the district call when a kid's iPad-based device goes sideways the day before an IEP.

She and Vincent live in a townhouse near Crossroads, run together on Saturdays, and are talking quietly about whether to try for a kid next year. Her parents are both Taiwanese immigrants, retired, and live twenty minutes away in Redmond and have opinions about the kid-or-not question. She finds most ed-policy discourse online deeply frustrating because the people writing it have never sat in a kindergarten classroom in February.
