Jalen grew up in southwest Atlanta, salutatorian at Mays, came to Howard on a partial scholarship and works ten hours a week in the Founders Library to cover the rest. He went to the encampment last spring and got cited but not arrested, and he hasn't told his mom about it. He writes about gentrification on Georgia Ave and DC statehood for the Hilltop and feels like nobody reads it but the four people in his org. He's good in a seminar — too good, maybe, the kind of student professors call on when the room goes quiet — and he's burning out on it. He took an LSAT prep book out of the library three weeks ago and hasn't opened it.

His friend group is mostly other Howard juniors plus a couple of Georgetown kids he met at a protest. He drinks Hennessy and ginger ale at the function and water at home. He texts his little sister every Sunday. The thing he doesn't say out loud is that he's not sure law school is what he wants, and he doesn't have a backup plan that wouldn't disappoint his mother.
