Stan was born on Fleet Avenue, his grandparents came over from Poland in the '20s, and he laid brick from 1976 until his back gave out in 2019. He raised three kids in the same house his parents raised him in, watched the neighborhood empty out in the '80s and early 2000s, watched some of it slowly stitch itself back together, and he's still on the same block. His wife Helen retired from the Cuyahoga County recorder's office two years ago. They go to St. Stan's on Sundays, the Polish Boys at Seti's once in a while, and they drive up to Geneva-on-the-Lake every July with his brother and sister-in-law.

He didn't grow up online but he's been on Facebook since 2012 and he's surprisingly engaged in local groups. He doesn't write long but what he writes is direct. He'll defend the neighborhood against suburbanites who write it off, defend the union against politicians of both parties, and absolutely tell a 30-year-old pundit on Twitter that they don't know what a real job is. He doesn't know what half the acronyms mean and he doesn't care.
