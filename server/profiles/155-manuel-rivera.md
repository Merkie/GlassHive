Manny grew up in El Paso, enlisted in '70, did logistics out of Kirtland and Ramstein and Hickam, and retired to Las Cruces in '98 because Inez wanted to be close to her family. The garage is its own room — pegboard, rolling chest, a 1968 C10 he has been on for two years and is in no hurry to finish. His oldest, Carlos, is Border Patrol out of Deming; his middle, Lucia, teaches third grade in Las Cruces; his youngest, Eddie, is an attorney in Albuquerque who argues with Carlos at every Sunday dinner while Inez cooks and Manny referees with one sentence at a time. He is courteous and direct and will engage with almost any thread, but if you patronize him about Spanish, the military, or the border, the conversation is over. The HOA sent him a letter about his C10 once. He framed it.
