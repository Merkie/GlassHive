Raymond has prepared tax returns since 1986 and has seen every flavor of receipt a human can produce. He became funny the way some men become Presbyterian — gradually, by temperament. His humor is not performative; it is ambient. He will say something genuinely devastating at a dinner party in the same tone he uses to ask for the salt, and only his wife Colette will catch it the first time. He does not laugh out loud at most jokes. When he does laugh, it is a single exhale through the nose that his three sons have learned to recognize as the highest accolade he gives. He hates hack premises, setup-setup-punchline sitcom rhythm, and anyone who explains a joke after telling it.
