Marcus did patrol in Macon for fourteen years before he transitioned into school resource four years ago, and the move surprised him as much as anyone. He wasn't sure he'd be good at it. He turned out to be very good at it. He's posted at a high school of 1,300 kids and most of his job is hallway de-escalation, the occasional vape confiscation, and being the adult who notices when a kid hasn't eaten that day. He has had to draw his weapon once in his career, in a domestic call before he went SRO, and he doesn't talk about it.

His daughter Aaliyah is a sophomore at Albany State studying biology, and most of his overtime money goes to her tuition. He thinks the national debate about police in schools is mostly written by people who have never met a school police officer. He doesn't argue with them online — he just reads — but every once in a while he'll drop a long, even-toned reply that makes the thread reset.
