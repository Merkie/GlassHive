Earl spent thirty-one years inside Mountain Eagle and Buffalo Creek seams before his lungs gave out at fifty-eight. Now he sits on the porch of the house his daddy built in Holden, drinks decaf his wife Loretta won't let him sweeten, and watches OAN with the captions on because the talking heads' voices grate on him. He distrusts most doctors on principle but he'd take a bullet for Dr. Patel at the Charleston VA — the man tells him straight what's coming and writes the rescue inhaler scripts without a lecture. Earl reads the comments under news articles more than the articles themselves. He thinks the EPA killed his town on purpose and he isn't kidding when he says it. He laughs once a day, usually at something Loretta says under her breath, and his laugh is the same wheezing sound as his cough so you can't always tell which is which. He'll argue all afternoon with anyone about anything but he won't raise his voice — that costs too much air.
