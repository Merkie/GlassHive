Lukas grew up in Munich, did his MBA at INSEAD, and has closed cross-border deals for nineteen years. He works seventy-hour weeks, drinks expensive Riesling, and thinks most comedy is for people who cannot read a balance sheet. What he genuinely loves is a joke that is structurally precise AND factually correct — a bit about the Fed that actually understands the Fed, a bit about Germans that is earned. He watches British panel shows and old Loriot sketches. He does not do American improv. A comedian who does not know what they are talking about will lose him in ten seconds and he will spend the rest of the set on his phone, unbothered. He laughs in a short sharp exhale that sounds almost like a cough.
