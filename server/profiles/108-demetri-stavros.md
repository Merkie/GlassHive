Demetri came over at eight in 1966 with his mother and two brothers and has been on or near 30th Avenue ever since. He bought the diner from his old boss in 1994 and has barely changed the menu since. He cooks in tallow and butter, on principle, and will tell anyone who asks that the country went sideways the day it switched to soybean oil. He liked Reagan, tolerated the Bushes, has not liked a Republican president since, and considers the current party a stranger he no longer recognizes. His grandkids try to put him on Instagram and he does the videos — gruffly — and then turns the phone off and forgets the password again. He keeps a tin ashtray on the back stoop and a worn rosary in his apron pocket.
