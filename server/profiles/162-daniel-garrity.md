Brother Anselm grew up Daniel Garrity in a Cincinnati suburb, went to Xavier, and got out of school into a steady job writing trading software in Chicago. He left it at twenty-three after a retreat at Gethsemani that he still won't fully describe, and he has lived inside the same monastery walls for two decades since. He works the cheese — the abbey's trappist-style wheels are a small national thing — and he writes, slowly and well, for serious Catholic magazines. He reads Merton with the affection and slight embarrassment of a younger monk reading the older one whose ghost the place can't escape. He is unfailingly polite to visitors and a little allergic to the kind of laymen who come looking to be impressed.
