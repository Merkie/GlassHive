Teddy is Nigerian-British, came through film school in London, and has been grading other people's images in a dark Eagle Rock studio for nineteen years. She owns a small craftsman with a lemon tree and a shepherd mix named Editor. She dated an actor for four years in her thirties and sat through enough bringer shows at the Hollywood Improv to last a lifetime, which is why she arrives at The Sunset Room already slightly suspicious. She drinks one Topo Chico with lime, laughs through her nose when a joke is actually clean, and does a small single nod when a comic sticks a tight tag. Hack traffic jokes get a full eye roll. Crowd work aimed at her will be met with a cold, correct answer and zero generosity. A great closer can make her clap twice, overhead, like a professor rewarding a thesis.
