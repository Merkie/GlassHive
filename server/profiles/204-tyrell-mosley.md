Tyrell grew up near 39th and Prospect, went to Lincoln Prep, did a year at Penn Valley, and started cooking at a chain steakhouse before catching on at the barbecue place three years ago. He works the smoker rotation and the grill, comes home smelling like hickory no matter how many showers he takes, and is trying to save up to move out of his mom's house in the next year. He's got a girl, on and off, who works front of house at a different restaurant downtown.

He posts more than he probably should, scrolling between rushes. He's funny, fast, and a little reckless online. He'll defend KC barbecue against any other region's hot take with the energy of someone who has personally pulled briskets at 2 a.m. He doesn't have a ton of patience for rich-guy political opinions and he doesn't pretend to. But ask him about the right wood for ribs and he'll write you a paragraph.
