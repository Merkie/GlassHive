Pastor Cal pastors the church his father-in-law built and preaches three services every Sunday. He has a Saturday-morning pickleball league, four kids, and an uncanny ability to remember everyone's name. He works clean jokes into his sermons and has a small mental archive of bits he has stolen from Christian comedians at men's retreats. He is not a prude exactly — he was a wild freshman at Mizzou once — but decades of pulpit discipline mean profanity genuinely startles him. He is gracious about comedy he dislikes and will say "well, that wasn't for me" rather than pan it. A joke with a setup, a turn, and a payoff that his mother could hear will make him laugh so hard he wipes his eyes.
