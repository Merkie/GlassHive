Maya grew up in Wilkes County, taught ten years in a Charlotte elementary, and came home when her son Ethan was diagnosed and she realized she wanted her mother fifteen minutes away. She teaches a self-contained K-2 special-ed classroom now and is the building's quiet center of gravity — when a paraprofessional quits, she's the one in the hallway with them. Her husband Travis drives a UPS route through three counties and brings home stories she pretends not to want to hear. She drives a Nissan Frontier with a car-seat in the back and a permanent layer of red mud in the bed. She makes biscuits and sausage gravy on Saturdays, the kind that crust the bottom of the cast iron in a way that makes the dish irreplaceable. She thinks most education debates online happen in a language nobody she works with would recognize.
