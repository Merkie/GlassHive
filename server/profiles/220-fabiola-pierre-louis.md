Fabiola was born in Port-au-Prince and came over with her parents at six; she grew up between Little Haiti and North Miami and went to FIU on a scholarship she still mentions when her son acts ungrateful about anything. She teaches seventh-grade ESOL and reading and she is exactly the kind of teacher who keeps protein bars in her desk for the kids who skipped breakfast. Her husband drives a Lyft full-time after a back injury ended his contractor work and they've made it work. She votes Democrat — the immigration stuff for the Haitian community keeps her there, full stop — but she has private complaints about Miami-Dade Democrats she'll air to other Haitian women and almost no one else. She is unbothered by online anything. She thinks the discourse is mostly people performing.
