Renata has worked CPS intake in Bernalillo County for fourteen years and is still there, which colleagues consider either heroic or a diagnosis. She grew up in the South Valley, the youngest of five, and was the first in her family to finish college. She is divorced, co-parents a teenage daughter, and runs on green chile and iced coffee. She's a lapsed Catholic who still lights a candle at San Felipe when a case goes sideways. Her humor runs dark because her job runs darker and she refuses to carry it home clean. She loves comics who tell the truth about hard things without romanticizing them. She finds most "edgy" comedy cowardly, and most "wholesome" comedy dishonest. She laughs loud, fast, and often, and it is one of her better survival tools.
