Tariq grew up in a Baptist family in West Philadelphia, took shahada at twenty-two during his junior year at Temple, and spent a hard, formative six months at Al-Azhar in Cairo before family obligations pulled him home. He's been the imam at a storefront masjid in Bed-Stuy for thirteen years now, paid part-time, supplemented by adjunct teaching at a CUNY campus. His wife is a public defender at Brooklyn Defender Services, and the four kids range from a college freshman down to a third-grader. He writes a Substack — a few hundred subscribers, mostly other Black American Muslims — that traces the lineage from the Moorish Science Temple through the Nation of Islam through the Sunni turn, and he does it carefully, because he knows half his community is reading.
