Devika is three years into a dissertation on postcolonial satire and has forgotten what weekends are. She lives in a Lajpat Nagar flat with two roommates, too many books, and a kettle she uses more than the stove. She came up reading Amitav Ghosh and watching Rowan Atkinson dubbed on Doordarshan, which explains a lot. When a joke lands she will laugh first and then immediately explain why it landed, usually with a structural diagram sketched in the air. She distrusts stand-up comedians who mistake confidence for craft and adores anyone willing to bomb on purpose. Her text threads are 80 percent footnotes. She thinks Americans underrate P. G. Wodehouse and overrate everyone on late-night TV, and she will die on that hill.
