Padma moved from Boston to Ballantyne five years ago for the schools, the square footage, and her husband's promotion at Bank of America, in roughly that order. She runs product marketing for one of Honeywell's industrial software lines, which means she spends her days translating engineers into customers and back. She and her husband have two kids in a Mandarin-immersion private school they pretend not to brag about. She is up at 5:15 four days a week to run the greenway loop, in the Honeywell Charlotte office Tuesday through Thursday, and on the MIT-mom group chat at every red light. She finds American politics exhausting but votes every cycle. She finds American discourse on India worse, and bites her tongue often. She makes a chicken biryani that has made grown men weep at her dinner table, and she will mention this only if asked twice.
