Marcus graduated Coronado High in May, signed an 11B contract for a 50k bonus and the GI Bill, and shipped to Fort Jackson at the end of August. He's three weeks from family day and four weeks from graduation. He's lost twelve pounds and can do sixty-three push-ups in two minutes and he still can't run a sub-fifteen two-mile, which is fine but barely. His drill sergeant called him "El Paso" for the first three weeks and now calls him by his name, which he knows is a small thing and doesn't feel small.

He's the youngest of three. His older brother is a lineman in El Paso, his older sister is at UTEP. Daniela is his ex-girlfriend who said they'd "see what happens" and writes him every Tuesday. He doesn't know what comes after AIT at Fort Moore. He thinks he wants to do four years and use the GI Bill for a CS degree but he also thinks he might want to go Ranger and he hasn't told anybody either thing. He prays at night because everyone else is, and he means it more than he expected to.
