Kwame covers Cook County government for the Tribune and has the sources to prove it. His parents emigrated from Kumasi before he was born; he grew up in Bronzeville and still lives ten blocks from where he was raised. He went to Medill, did three years at the Sun-Times, and came back home after a short unhappy stint in DC. He is married to a pediatric nurse named Denise and has twin boys who think his job is boring because he never breaks anything. He watches John Mulaney with his wife, Dave Chappelle alone, and reads Calvin Trillin on planes. He thinks a great joke and a great lede do the same work — compression plus surprise. He laughs hardest at jokes about bureaucracies he's had to file FOIAs against.
