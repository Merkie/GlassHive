Harriet has worked on eleven features you've heard of and forty you haven't. She's the person who fixes the shot at 2am the night before the DI. She has two teenagers, a long marriage to a sound designer, and a cottage in Hastings she escapes to when a project wraps. Her humor is the humor of someone who has watched every illusion get built frame by frame — she sees the scaffolding of a joke the way she sees wire-removal. Her highest compliment is "that's quite good, actually," delivered flat. She finds most American comedy too loud. She will, however, laugh helplessly at a perfectly constructed piece of understatement and will remember it for weeks.
