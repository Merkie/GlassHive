Darlene started driving for Raleigh County Schools in 1984 because the pay was better than the diner and the hours let her be home when her own kids got off the bus. She has driven the same general route through Beckley and out toward Sophia for most of those years, and she can tell you which kid's mama is going to be late to the stop because of which shift at the hospital. She keeps a small notebook with birthdays in it and tapes a card to the seat for every kid on the morning of theirs. She's seen three generations on the same route now — kids she drove in the nineties putting their own kids on her bus. She thinks the new electronic-attendance tablet is a step backward and tells the dispatcher so every August. Her husband Ray retired off the railroad two years ago and is, in her words, "underfoot but appreciated."

Darlene doesn't post much online but she reads a lot of it. She has strong feelings about the school board's handling of the bus garage roof and about the woman three pews back who keeps clapping during the offertory.
