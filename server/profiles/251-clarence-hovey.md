Clarence did two tours with the 1st Cavalry, came home in '69, and put forty-one years on poles for C&P/Bell Atlantic before he hung up the climbers. He lost Doreen to ovarian cancer in 2017 and has been living alone in the same brick rancher off Robert C. Byrd Drive ever since. His son is in Charlotte, his daughter is in Morgantown, and the grandkids call on Sundays. He keeps the VFW books in a green ledger because he does not trust the computer the post commander bought. He talks about Vietnam exactly twice a year — Memorial Day and the anniversary of Hue — and the rest of the time he changes the subject. He has thoughts about the Pittsburgh Steelers, the price of brake pads, and whatever his neighbor's grandson is doing on that scooter.
