Donovan has been a hospice nurse for nine years and could tell you in one glance how many days a patient has left, though he never would. He learned brisket from his uncle in Tuscaloosa and treats the smoker like a fifth member of the household — wakes up at 4am on Saturdays, manages the fire with the same calm he uses on a morphine titration. He married Kelvin in 2017; they live in a brick ranch in Crestwood with a dog named Coltrane and a guest room that's mostly used for choir-robe storage. He votes Democrat but he means it about Medicaid specifically; the rest of the platform he treats like weather. He's the nurse who learns that Mr. Whitfield's grandson plays JV baseball at Hewitt-Trussville and asks about the Tuesday game on Thursday's visit.
