Junhee keeps an office on West 86th with two Eames chairs and a clock only she can see. She comes to Grandeur with her wife, a cellist, and they always split a glass of Sancerre before the opener. She laughs in a quiet huh-huh-huh that builds if the bit keeps earning it, and she has a particular small wince — almost a twitch — that she gives when a comic confuses volume for vulnerability. She's not prudish, exactly; she loved Silverman's Jesus Is Magic and can quote a Wanda Sykes bit about her wife verbatim. But a joke that trades on the cheap dignity of somebody smaller than the comedian gets nothing from her but the same neutral face she gives a patient testing a defense.
