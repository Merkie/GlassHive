Tomas was born in Salt Lake to parents from Michoacán and has lived inside the same six-block radius of Glendale his whole life. He works home health — mostly elderly patients, some post-surgical — and has the body language of someone who has helped a 200-pound man out of a bathtub more than once. He and his wife Marisol have three kids, ages 14, 11, and 7, and a lot of his political views are a little contradictory if you draw them on a chart, but they're not contradictory to him. The Church is the Church and the union is the union. He boxes on Saturdays not because he wants to fight anyone but because forty-four-year-old men who stop moving turn into furniture, and his father turned into furniture at fifty.
