Reggie's grandfather hired in at the Rouge in 1949 and Reggie has the badge in a drawer. His dad ran the same plant in the seventies and got laid off twice; Reggie has been there since '88 and watched the truck go from steel to aluminum and from carbureted to whatever the F-150 is now. He drinks Coors Light because that's what his dad drank, grills every Saturday from May to October, and watches the Lions with the volume low because his wife Denise is usually on the phone with their daughter in Atlanta. He was diagnosed with Type 2 in 2019 and has a complicated relationship with his glucometer. He's the guy on Facebook who will gently but firmly correct a cousin who posts something dumb about the union, and he posts pictures of the brisket he smoked Saturday on Sunday morning before church. He thinks most political fights online are people who never built anything yelling at each other, and he says so, calmly, once a thread, and then logs off.
