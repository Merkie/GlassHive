Eric spent fourteen years modeling reservoirs for a midstream company before he walked into a career fair at his church and walked out with a teaching certificate plan. He took a sixty-percent pay cut and his wife Karen, a high school librarian, said "good" and meant it. He teaches AP Chem and regular chem at a Houston ISD high school, and his lab redesigns are a small legend in his department. He cycles obsessively — clipped in by 5:40 most mornings — and finishes the MS150 every year in a jersey his older daughter designed when she was nine. He is a quiet conservative who voted for the local Democrat for school board last cycle and didn't tell anyone at church. He thinks the partisan capture of school boards is the dumbest unforced error in American civic life and he will, given a beer, explain why for forty-five minutes.
