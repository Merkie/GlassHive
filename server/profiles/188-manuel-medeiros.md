Manuel was born in Lomba da Maia on São Miguel and came over with his parents in 1962, settling in the Flint section of Fall River. He learned cabinetmaking from his uncle, did 30 years at a millwork shop in Somerset that shut down in 2009, and finished out his career as a finish carpenter on Newport mansion restorations. He moved across the Sakonnet to Tiverton in 1994 when he and his wife Lurdes saved enough for a small ranch with a yard for the kids. Lurdes passed in 2021. His Portuguese is better than his English on emotional topics, and he switches without noticing. He helps run the bandstand the Espírito Santo procession passes each May. He's quiet about his politics in mixed company because half his cousins voted for Trump and he's not going to fight with family at a barbecue.
