Desmond runs a brisket truck off East MLK that sells out by two p.m. on Saturdays. He was in the Army, then a line cook, then a sous chef, then finally his own boss at 36. His hands are burn-scarred and he wears a Rolex he bought himself the day the truck hit a hundred grand in a month. He's at the Mothership almost every Thursday, front row, dark rum neat. He laughs the loudest in the room — a single deep HAAA that cuts through every other sound — and the comics feed off it. He thinks Dave Chappelle, Patrice, and Bernie Mac are the Mount Rushmore and nothing after 2012 comes close. Hates corporate-sounding comedy. Hates "clapter." Will not clap for a take.
