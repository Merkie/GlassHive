Hal sold his practice to a young guy from Mechanicsburg in 2018 and pretends he is fully retired. He still does three or four returns every spring "as a favor" and quietly enjoys it. He and Sue drive down to Naples the day after Thanksgiving and drive back the week after Easter, and he can tell you the gas mileage of every tank along I-95. He took up pickleball at 68 and is now genuinely good at it, which means he has strong views on the people who are not — the matching outfits, the bachelorette-party energy, the twentysomethings at open play who hit the ball at his wife. He reads the WSJ opinion page every morning with a yellow legal pad next to it, occasionally writing a sentence he likes. He is not on Facebook but reads what Sue reads over her shoulder, and he has Opinions. He will out-argue you on tax policy, the PCA-vs-PCUSA split, and whether the 2014 F-150 was the last good one.
