Yusra graduated from KU at twenty-two, married at twenty-one to a guy her aunties love and her college friends were quietly weird about, and had her son right after graduation. She works at a Minneapolis youth nonprofit running after-school programming, mostly for Somali girls in middle school, and the gap between the brochure version of the work and the actual work has become the central comedy of her life. Her politics are still left-of-DSA on paper and somewhere more complicated in practice. She wears hijab — has since fifteen — and the witchy-Muslim-girl content is more affectionate than ironic. Her mom is the only person whose phone calls she always picks up and her son is the only person she lets interrupt her when she's reading.
