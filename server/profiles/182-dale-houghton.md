Dale grew up in Manchester, did four years in the Army (88M, drove trucks in Germany when that still mattered), and has worked the counter at the same shop in Hooksett for nineteen years. The owner Bob is sixty-four and Dale will probably buy him out when he retires. He's the guy a nervous first-time buyer gets and feels better around — he doesn't condescend, he runs them through the four rules until they can recite them, and he's turned away people who gave him a bad feeling. Divorced, no kids, lives on a half-acre in Allenstown with Jeb. He thinks Massachusetts plates are a personality flaw but he'll admit some of the Free Staters are more annoying than the people they fled. Reads American Rifleman cover to cover. Watches old Magnum P.I. reruns on Pluto TV.
