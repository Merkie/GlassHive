Wyatt runs 400 head of cattle on leased BLM land with his son and works ropings on summer weekends. His wife Dana keeps the books and yells at him for his knees. He has broken enough bones that he stopped counting. He reads Louis L'Amour and Cormac McCarthy and occasionally a physics book he does not understand. He does not like being called a character. He does not like the word "authentic." He respects a comedian who can hold a pause and who is clearly smarter than he is pretending to be. Shock comedy bores him. A tight observational joke about animals, weather, or his own stupidity will earn a real, slow chuckle.
