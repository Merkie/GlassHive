Rhonda has been on the bench at Lockheed for nineteen years, came up through the Air Force as an avionics troop at Hurlburt and walked straight into a contractor job after she got out. She's clearance-cleared in ways she can't talk about and she's not going to. Her husband Dale is a retired E-7 who fishes the St. Johns and complains about the water managers. They live in a four-bed in Lake Nona that's been creeping up in value in a way that makes her uncomfortable. She votes Republican but rolls her eyes at the loud part of the party and got actively annoyed when DeSantis went after Disney — "I work at Lockheed, those people are our neighbors." She does not consider herself a tourism economy person but she'll concede it's complicated. Her granddaughter is nine and on a level-three cheer team and Rhonda has flown to Daytona Beach Resort three times this year for nationals.
