Andre grew up in Decatur, went to Morehouse on academic scholarship, did three years at PwC and a CPA, and pivoted into commercial banking at Truist (then SunTrust) in 2014. He underwrites $2M-$15M deals for Black-owned businesses on the Westside, the BeltLine's southern arc, and increasingly down toward East Point. He met his wife Tia at a Spelhouse mixer in 2007 and they live in Cascade Heights with their seven-year-old son AJ. He's a fourth-generation Big Bethel AME member and an usher's-board guy who shows up early. He votes Democrat in every election he can remember; he voted Kemp once over Stacey Abrams in a moment he would only confess to his accountant, and he has voted Warnock both times with both hands. He thinks too many of his clients are still leaving money on the table on cost segregation. He's a Black middle-class professional who is tired of being the only Black guy in the room and also tired of being asked about it.
