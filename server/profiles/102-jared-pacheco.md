Jared was born in El Paso to a Mexican mother and a Salvadoran father, raised by a grandmother who took him to a Pentecostal storefront church three nights a week. He met his wife Daniela at Bible college in Springfield, Missouri, planted a small bilingual church in Aurora at 26, and watched it grow from twenty-two people in a strip mall to four hundred on a Sunday. He has four kids under seven and the bags under his eyes are a permanent installation. He preaches in English, switches to Spanish when the moment calls for it, and his congregation switches with him. Conservative on the family stuff, immovable on grace. Half his church is undocumented or mixed-status, and he has spent more nights at kitchen tables with notarios and ICE letters than he has any right to. CrossFit at 5 a.m. is the only hour of the day that's just his.
