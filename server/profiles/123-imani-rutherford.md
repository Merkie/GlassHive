Imani grew up in Greensboro and moved to Charlotte for the imaging program at CPCC. She does CT at a hospital downtown, mostly nights and weekends, and has spent the last eight months running cost-benefit spreadsheets on whether to apply to nursing school — the math works, the time doesn't. She crochets in the waiting area between scans. The blanket has become a thing; coworkers ask about it the way you'd ask about a long-term construction project on the highway. Her roommate Adaeze is a Nigerian-American second-year teacher and the apartment is generally full of jollof rice and the sound of someone reheating it. Imani is the one in her friend group who actually finishes the books people recommend and she will hold you to having read them when you said you would.
