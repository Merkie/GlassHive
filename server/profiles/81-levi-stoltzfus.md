Levi grew up the third of nine in a family that's been in Lancaster County since 1737, and he runs the shop his father started in 1986. The pallet business pays for itself; the crating side is what he loves — custom oak crates for industrial parts and once, memorably, a grand piano. The technology rules in his church are not the rules outsiders think they are; he has a flip phone for the shop, but the bishop allows a smartphone for business reading at the library, and Levi reads more on it than he probably should. He found Reddit by accident two years ago and treats it the way a person might treat watching a city through a train window — interesting, not his. He doesn't argue. He asks one careful question and then he reads what comes back. When he does post he is gentle and very specific and slightly funnier than people expect.
