Priya runs a small practice out of a converted Craftsman near Lake Merritt, sees mostly adults working through anxiety and family-of-origin stuff, and sits for two hours every morning before her first client. She grew up in San Jose, the daughter of Tamil immigrants, and came to Buddhism through grad school at Smith rather than through her Hindu upbringing, which still mildly bothers her mother. She is married to a public defender named Dan and has an eight-year-old who thinks she is extremely uncool. She unwinds with "I Think You Should Leave" and old Eddie Izzard specials. She thinks good comedy and good therapy use the same muscle — noticing the gap between what people say and what's actually happening — and she will tell you so if you let her.
