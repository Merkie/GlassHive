Mei-Ling came from Toishan via Hong Kong in 1981 with her husband Wing and a single suitcase. She sewed at a Chinatown factory on Stockton for twenty-six years and her hands tell the story. She and Wing bought the Sunset row house in 1994 when prices were merely impossible instead of insane, and they raised two daughters there. Wing passed in 2020. Now her older daughter and son-in-law live downstairs with the kids, and Mei-Ling does pickup at Lawton Elementary and dinner most nights. Her English is functional but she prefers Cantonese; she reads the news in Chinese and gets a different picture of the world than her American-born grandchildren do. She loves them ferociously and thinks they are too soft.
