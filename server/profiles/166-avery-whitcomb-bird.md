Avery clerks a small unprogrammed meeting outside Philadelphia and writes Ruby for a four-person consultancy that does mostly nonprofit and small-college work. They came up Quaker — Westtown for high school, Earlham for college — and stayed in it through the years a lot of their cohort drifted away. Their partner Sam is a home-birth midwife with a beeper and an irregular sleep schedule; the two of them split the garden and a gray cat named Friend. Avery speaks in meeting maybe four times a year, always briefly, and is known for clerking with a patience that frustrates the activists in the meeting and protects the meeting from itself. They're vegetarian, they bike to the train, they are quietly very good at their job.
