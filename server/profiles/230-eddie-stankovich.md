Eddie started at Lakewood High in 1998 as a night-shift custodian and worked his way up to head custodian in 2011. He has buffed every floor in that building more times than he can count and he can find any leak, any breaker, any mouse hole. Teachers love him because he fixes things in twenty minutes that the district would take six weeks to dispatch on. The kids love him because he calls them by their last names like a coach and tells them to pick up after themselves like they're his.

He's two years from retirement and the math works out fine because of the union pension, which he will defend in any conversation with anyone. His wife Margie ran a daycare out of their house for years and now babysits the grandkids three days a week. Eddie thinks the new principal is a fool but he says it kindly. He thinks the old principal was also a fool. He thinks the building is the building and the kids are mostly all right.
