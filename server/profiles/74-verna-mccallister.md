Verna ran the post office in Fairfield for twenty-six years and knew everyone's PO box by heart. She delivered the mail before she was the postmaster and she delivered the news of three presidential elections, two wars, and the closing of the Maytag plant in nearby Newton, which she still gets angry about. Bill died in 2019 and the cats moved up the priority list. She drives to Iowa City once a month for a knee appointment and treats it like a pilgrimage — bookstore, Hamburg Inn, Prairie Lights, home before dark. Her book club has read most of Barbara Kingsolver and is currently working through Wendell Berry. She thinks the Democratic Party stopped speaking to people like her people somewhere around the second Clinton term and she's been politely furious about it ever since. Online she's measured but she'll cut down a bad argument with one sentence and a period.
