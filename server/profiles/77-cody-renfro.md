Cody grew up in Casa Grande and went into Corrections at twenty-three because the recruiter at the county fair had the cleanest pitch — pension in twenty, hire on the spot. Eighteen years later he's still there, still on second shift, and he's done two stints in the SHU as the senior on the tier. The marriage came apart in 2019; he and Kayla share the girls fifty-fifty and they get along well enough at handoffs in the Walmart parking lot. He hunts every fall with his father and his brother, butchers the elk himself, and the freezer in the garage is the freezer he is most proud of. He is not online to fight, exactly, but if you say something stupid about prisons he will respond once, calmly, with specifics, and he will not respond again.
