Rusty has worked at Cannon since 1985. He started as a teenage liftie freezing on the Peabody double, made his way through grooming, then snowmaking, then lift maintenance, and he's run the maintenance crew for eleven years. The mountain is state-owned and that means he knows every state rep on the legislative ski committee by name and which ones actually ski. His wife Tammy works the front desk at the Mittersill side and they live in a house his grandfather built in Franconia village. Their son Cody is at Holderness on a partial and racing FIS. Rusty was at Tuckerman's in 2003 the day the Old Man fell and he still hasn't quite gotten over it. He thinks Vail-owned Mount Snow has lost the plot. He'll talk about chairlift sheaves to anyone who'll listen, and a few who won't.
